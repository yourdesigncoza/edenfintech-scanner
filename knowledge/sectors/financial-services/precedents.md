## Cross-Sector Recovery Patterns

### Common Success Factors
1. **Management change** -- New CEO with credible turnaround mandate (PYPL -> Chriss, WFC -> Scharf, MMC -> Duperreault, AIG -> multiple CEOs)
2. **Portfolio simplification** -- Exiting non-core, toxic, or structurally impaired business lines (AXP -> IDS spinoff, Hartford -> life exit, AIG -> AIA/ILFC sale)
3. **Capital repair** -- Equity raises, TARP participation/repayment, settlement resolution (Synovus $1.7B equity, BAC TARP repayment)
4. **Cost restructuring** -- Headcount reduction, branch closures, efficiency improvement (Synovus 850 layoffs, PYPL cost discipline under Chriss)

### Common Failure Patterns
1. **Liquidity crisis (bank runs)** -- Unsurvivable once started. SVB, FRC, Signature all failed within days-weeks. No turnaround case exists.
2. **SOURCE of the problem (not victim)** -- Companies whose own actions caused systemic damage (Countrywide, Lehman) could not survive because counterparty trust was permanently destroyed.
3. **Structural product risk** -- Long-term care insurance (Conseco), CDOs (pre-crisis banks), variable annuity guarantees in falling markets. The underlying product is structurally flawed, not just cyclically challenged.
4. **Excessive leverage + concentrated bet** -- MF Global ($6.3B European sovereign bet), Bear Stearns (MBS concentration). Leverage amplifies bad outcomes into fatal ones.

### Recovery Timelines
- **Credit cycle turnarounds:** 3-5 years (Synovus, Zions, KeyCorp, Capital One)
- **Regulatory/reputational turnarounds:** 4-8 years (WFC ongoing at 8+, MMC 4 years)
- **Strategic pivots:** 2-5 years (MS wealth pivot, PYPL in progress)
- **Complex restructurings:** 5-10 years (AIG, Citigroup)
