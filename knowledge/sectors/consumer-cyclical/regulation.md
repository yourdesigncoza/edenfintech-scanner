# Consumer Discretionary -- Regulatory Landscape

## Regulatory Bodies

| Regulator | Jurisdiction | Discretion Level | Key Sub-sectors Affected |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| NHTSA (Nat'l Highway Traffic Safety Admin) | Vehicle and auto parts safety standards (FMVSS) | Moderate -- rules-based standards, discretionary enforcement timing | Auto Parts, Auto Manufacturers, Auto Dealerships |
| EPA (Environmental Protection Agency) | Vehicle emissions, fuel economy, environmental compliance | Moderate -- rules-based standards, discretionary in penalties | Auto Parts, Auto Manufacturers, Residential Construction |
| CPSC (Consumer Product Safety Commission) | Consumer product safety (apparel flammability, children's products) | Moderate -- mandatory standards + discretionary recall authority | Apparel, Furnishings, Leisure, Personal Products |
| FTC (Federal Trade Commission) | Unfair/deceptive trade practices, labeling, pricing, junk fees | High -- broad Section 5 authority is inherently discretionary | All sub-sectors (advertising, pricing, labeling) |
| FDA (Food and Drug Administration) | Cosmetics, personal care products safety and labeling | Moderate -- MoCRA 2022 expanded authority significantly | Personal Products & Services |
| DOT (Dept of Transportation) | Airline and travel service regulation | Moderate | Travel Services |
| State Gaming Commissions | Casino licensing, AML compliance, player protection | High -- discretionary licensing and enforcement | Gambling, Resorts & Casinos |
| State/Local Health Departments | Restaurant food safety, hygiene, facility standards | Moderate -- rules-based codes, discretionary inspections | Restaurants |
| OSHA (Occupational Safety & Health Admin) | Workplace safety across all sub-sectors | Moderate | All sub-sectors with physical operations |
| DOL (Department of Labor) | Labor law compliance (wages, hours, workplace conditions) | Moderate | Restaurants, Retail, Travel Lodging |
| CBP (Customs and Border Protection) | Import duties, tariffs, anti-dumping enforcement | Rules-based with tariff policy discretion | Apparel, Auto Parts, Specialty Retail |

## Key Frameworks

**Rules-Based (Predictable):**
- FMVSS (Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards) -- manufacturer self-certification required
- Flammable Fabrics Act -- specific flammability standards for clothing textiles
- FDA cosmetics labeling requirements -- ingredient disclosure mandated
- State health codes for restaurants -- based on FDA Food Code

**Discretionary (Less Predictable):**
- FTC Section 5 enforcement -- broad "unfair or deceptive" standard applied case-by-case
- State gaming commission licensing decisions -- discretionary revocation authority
- EPA enforcement priorities -- selective targeting of violations
- NHTSA recall timing -- discretionary investigation initiation

**Hybrid:**
- Tariff policy -- rules-based collection but policy decisions are executive/legislative and unpredictable
- Right-to-repair legislation -- state-by-state, evolving rapidly

## Binary Regulatory Risks

1. **NHTSA mandatory recall** -- Can force costly recalls without warning. Ford's $165M penalty shows scale. Takata shows existential risk for suppliers.
2. **Tariff shock** -- 25% tariffs on imported auto parts (May 2025) and 50% on steel/aluminum imposed via executive action. No advance warning or transition period.
3. **Gaming license revocation** -- State commissions can revoke licenses discretionarily. AML violations are the primary trigger (Caesars, MGM, Wynn fines in 2025 signal intensifying enforcement).
4. **FTC junk fee rules** -- New rule requires mandatory fees in travel/lodging to be shown upfront. Implementation disrupts booking revenue models.
5. **MoCRA cosmetics regulation** -- FDA gained mandatory recall authority for cosmetics in 2022. First major expansion of FDA cosmetics authority since 1938.

## Upcoming Changes (2025-2027)

| Rule/Change | Agency | Expected Impact | Timeline |
|------------|--------|----------------|----------|
| Multi-Pollutant Emissions Standards (MY 2027+) | EPA | More protective GHG, NOx, PM standards for light/medium-duty vehicles | Finalized Mar 2024, phasing in MY 2027+ |
| Updated CAFE Standards | NHTSA/DOT | New fuel economy requirements for MY 2027-2032 | Finalized June 2024 |
| 25% Auto Parts Tariffs | Executive/USTR | Increases imported parts costs, disrupts supply chains | Effective May 3, 2025 |
| 50% Steel/Aluminum Tariffs | Executive/USTR | Doubles import costs on chassis, axle components | Effective June 4, 2025 |
| Right-to-Repair Laws | State legislatures | Expands independent repair access to OEM data/tools | Multiple states, 2025-2027 |
| FTC Junk Fee Rule | FTC | Upfront total pricing required for travel/lodging bookings | Effective 2025 |
| State Data Privacy Laws | State legislatures | Consumer data handling requirements for retail/travel | Multiple states, phased 2025-2027 |
