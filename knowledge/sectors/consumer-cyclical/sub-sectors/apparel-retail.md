# Apparel Retail

## Overview

Apparel retailers (Gap GPS, Victoria's Secret VSCO, American Eagle AEO, Abercrombie & Fitch ANF) operate branded store chains and e-commerce. Revenue driven by fashion trends, store traffic, and online conversion. High operating leverage from fixed lease costs means margins swing dramatically with same-store sales. The sector faces secular headwinds from e-commerce disruption, mall traffic decline, and fast-fashion competition.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Same-Store Sales Growth | Organic demand | 2-5% | Negative 3+ quarters |
| Gross Margin | Markdown discipline | 55-65% | <50% (over-promoting) |
| EBITDA Margin | Operating profitability | 10-16% | <6% |
| E-commerce Penetration | Channel shift progress | 25-40% | <15% with declining stores |
| Inventory/Sales Ratio | Markdown risk | Declining or stable | Rising >10% YoY |
| Store Traffic Trend | Physical demand | Stable | Declining >5% |
| Rent/Revenue | Occupancy leverage | 8-12% | >15% |
| Return on Invested Capital | Capital efficiency | >15% | <8% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2x | >3.5x |
| Customer Acquisition Cost | Marketing efficiency | Stable or declining | Rising >20% YoY |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 8-14x. Higher for growth/premium brands (ANF at peak), lower for challenged operators (GPS).
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Ignoring lease liabilities (IFRS 16 / ASC 842 inflates EBITDA). (2) Valuing turnaround stories on peak-cycle margins.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Abercrombie & Fitch | 2016-2023 | Brand fatigue, declining comp sales, CEO departure | Rebrand targeting older demo, Hollister refresh, cost discipline | Successful | 5-6 years | Stock from $8 (2017) to $180+ (2024) |
| Gap Inc. | 2020-present | Multi-brand decline, Old Navy missteps, Yeezy deal collapse | CEO changes, brand simplification, cost cuts | In progress -- early positive signs | Ongoing | Stock volatile, $8-$30 range |
| J.Crew | 2017-2020 | Overleveraged, brand decline, store closures | Chapter 11 (May 2020), emerged Sept 2020, debt restructuring | Partial -- private, still rebuilding | 3+ years | N/A (private) |

**Pattern:** Apparel retail turnarounds are rare and slow. Brand relevance must be salvageable. ANF is the standout success but took 6 years. 1 of 3 fully successful. Base rate ~40%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational
- **Cyclicality:** Moderate to High
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Occasional (lease covenant violations, liquidity crises)

### Kill Factors

1. **Brand irrelevance + high leverage** -- combination of declining brand and debt load is fatal (J.Crew, Express)
2. **Mall-dependent with no e-commerce strategy** -- secular headwind with no offset

### Friction Factors

1. **Fashion risk / trend misses** -- suggested friction: -1. One bad season can destroy a quarter.
2. **High fixed lease costs** -- suggested friction: 0 to -1. Operating leverage cuts both ways.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 2/5 -- Fashion trends are inherently unpredictable; brand equity is subjective.
**Suggested PCS friction:** 0 -- Primarily operational risk, well-understood.
