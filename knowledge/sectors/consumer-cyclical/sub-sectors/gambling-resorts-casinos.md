# Gambling, Resorts & Casinos

## Overview

Casino and gaming companies (MGM Resorts MGM, Caesars CZR, Wynn Resorts WYNN, Las Vegas Sands LVS, Boyd Gaming BYD) operate physical casinos and increasingly online sports betting/iGaming platforms. Revenue comes from gaming (60-70%), hotel (15-20%), and F&B/entertainment (15-20%). Capital intensity is extreme for land-based operations. Macau/Singapore exposure adds geopolitical risk. Online sports betting (DraftKings DKNG, Flutter/FanDuel FLUT) is a growth segment but remains unprofitable for many operators.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| EBITDA Margin | Property profitability | 30-40% (Las Vegas), 25-35% (regional) | <20% |
| RevPAR (Hotel) | Room revenue | Growing with inflation+ | Declining |
| Gaming Revenue/Available Position | Slot/table productivity | Growing | Declining |
| Occupancy Rate | Demand | 85-95% (Las Vegas) | <75% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <4x | >6x (common in sector) |
| Capex/Revenue | Reinvestment | 6-10% | >15% (major project underway) |
| Macau GGR Market Share | China exposure | Stable | Declining license renewal risk |
| Online Revenue Growth | Digital pivot | 20-40% annual | <10% with heavy investment |
| Player Reinvestment Rate | Marketing efficiency | 15-25% | >30% (overspending) |
| FCF After Maintenance Capex | True cash generation | Positive | Negative after maintenance |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 8-14x. Las Vegas Strip properties at premium (12-14x). Regional casinos at 6-9x. Online/iGaming valued on revenue multiples for growth-stage.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Ignoring rent expense for REIT-structured casino operators (OpCo/PropCo splits distort EBITDA). (2) Not adjusting for Macau concession renewal risk. (3) Applying mature multiples to unprofitable online sports betting.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Caesars | 2015-2021 | Chapter 11 (Jan 2015), $18B debt from leveraged buyout | Debt restructuring, emerged Oct 2017, merged with Eldorado 2020 | Successful -- emerged as largest US casino operator | 6 years including Ch.11 | Emerged at ~$10, rose to $120+ (2021) |
| MGM Resorts | 2008-2013 | Near-bankruptcy, CityCenter cost overruns, GFC collapse | Asset sales, OpCo/PropCo restructuring, cost cuts | Successful | 4-5 years | Stock from $2 (2009) to $25+ (2014) |
| Wynn Resorts | 2018-2021 | Founder scandal, Macau downturn, COVID closure | New leadership, Macau recovery, online entry | Partial | 3 years | Stock volatile, $50-130 range |

**Pattern:** Casino turnarounds work when underlying property demand recovers and debt is restructured. Chapter 11 is common for over-leveraged operators. 2 of 3 fully recovered. Base rate ~65%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical / Regulatory
- **Cyclicality:** High (leisure spending, convention/tourism traffic)
- **Regulatory discretion:** High (gaming licenses are discretionary, AML enforcement intensifying)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Occasional (license revocation, Macau concession non-renewal)

### Kill Factors

1. **Gaming license revocation** -- discretionary regulatory action that eliminates revenue base
2. **Macau concession non-renewal** -- for operators with >40% Macau revenue

### Friction Factors

1. **AML enforcement risk** -- suggested friction: -1 to -2. Caesars ($7.8M), MGM ($8.5M), Wynn ($11.25M), Resorts World ($10.5M) all fined in 2025.
2. **Macau/geopolitical risk** -- suggested friction: -1. China regulatory uncertainty.
3. **Extreme leverage** -- suggested friction: -1. Sector typically operates at 4-6x debt/EBITDA.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 2/5 -- Regulatory discretion (gaming licenses, AML) and geopolitical exposure (Macau) create high uncertainty.
**Suggested PCS friction:** -1 -- Regulatory + cyclical exposure.
