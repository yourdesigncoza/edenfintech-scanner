## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **EV Transition Impact** -- Affects auto manufacturers, OEM parts suppliers, and (eventually) aftermarket. ICE-component suppliers face existential risk while aftermarket benefits from aging ICE fleet in the medium term. Dealerships face potential disintermediation from direct-sales models.

2. **Tariff Disruption (2025)** -- 25% tariffs on auto parts, 50% on steel/aluminum, and ongoing apparel/consumer goods tariffs from China reshape supply chains across auto parts, apparel manufacturing, home improvement, and furnishings. Companies with US-based manufacturing or diversified sourcing benefit.

3. **Housing Cycle Sensitivity** -- Residential construction, home improvement, and furnishings/appliances all correlate with housing activity. Interest rates are the key variable. Current elevated rates suppress housing turnover but create pent-up demand.

4. **"Experiences Over Things" Secular Shift** -- Benefits travel (lodging, services, casinos), restaurants, and leisure at the expense of apparel retail and department stores. This trend survived COVID and appears structural.

5. **E-Commerce Disruption** -- Continues to pressure department stores and commodity-focused retailers. Off-price (TJX, ROST) and category specialists (Best Buy, Ulta) have proven resilient. Physical retail survivors need a defensible reason for the store visit.

6. **Post-COVID Normalization** -- Many sub-sectors experienced extreme demand swings (positive for home/RV/e-commerce, negative for travel/restaurants). Normalization is largely complete by 2025-2026 but left some companies (Peloton, Wayfair, cruises) with structural debt/demand hangovers.

## Macro Context (2025-2026)

- **Interest rates** remain elevated, suppressing housing turnover, auto purchases, and big-ticket discretionary spending. Aftermarket auto parts and repair services benefit (consumers hold cars longer).
- **Consumer confidence** is mixed -- employment remains strong but inflation fatigue affects discretionary spending in lower income brackets.
- **Tariff uncertainty** creates margin compression risk across supply-chain-intensive sub-sectors (auto parts, apparel, furnishings).
- **Average vehicle age** at record 12.8 years (2025), driving aftermarket parts demand.
- **Housing shortage** of 3-5 million units provides long-term support for homebuilders despite near-term rate headwinds.

## Inter-Sub-Sector Dynamics

- **Auto ecosystem** (Manufacturers <-> Parts <-> Dealerships <-> Recreational Vehicles): OEM production volumes cascade through the entire chain. Parts suppliers are most vulnerable to OEM concentration; dealerships are buffered by service revenue.
- **Housing ecosystem** (Residential Construction <-> Home Improvement <-> Furnishings): New home construction drives initial furnishing/appliance demand; existing home sales drive remodeling demand. All three correlate with mortgage rates.
- **Travel ecosystem** (Lodging <-> Travel Services <-> Gambling/Casinos <-> Restaurants): Tourism and business travel drive interconnected demand. Asset-light models (hotel franchises, OTAs) recover faster than asset-heavy (cruise lines, physical casinos).
- **Fashion/Retail ecosystem** (Apparel Manufacturers <-> Apparel Retail <-> Footwear <-> Department Stores <-> Specialty Retail): Brand strength determines whether a company is a winner or loser within the e-commerce disruption. Off-price and DTC channels are taking share from traditional wholesale/department store distribution.
