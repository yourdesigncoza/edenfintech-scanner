# Furnishings, Fixtures & Appliances

## Overview

Includes furniture (La-Z-Boy LZB, Ethan Allen ETD), appliances (Whirlpool WHR), home furnishings (RH, Williams-Sonoma WSM, Wayfair W), and fixtures/lighting companies. Revenue tied to housing activity (new homes, remodeling) and consumer discretionary spending. Ranges from luxury (RH, 18-22% EBITDA margin) to value (Wayfair, typically EBITDA-negative to breakeven). The sector was a major COVID beneficiary (nesting demand) followed by significant pullback as spending shifted back to services.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 12-22% (premium), 5-10% (mass) | <5% |
| Comparable Sales Growth | Organic demand | 2-5% | Negative 3+ quarters |
| Gross Margin | Pricing/sourcing | 35-50% | <30% |
| Order Backlog | Forward demand | Stable | Declining >20% |
| Housing Starts Correlation | Demand driver | Positive correlation | Diverging negatively |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2.5x | >4x |
| Inventory/Revenue | Working capital | 15-20% | >25% |
| E-commerce Penetration | Channel mix | 30-50%+ | <15% for non-store operators |
| Customer Retention | Loyalty | Repeat rate >30% | Declining |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 6-12% | <3% |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 8-14x. Premium brands (RH, WSM) at 14-18x. Mass/value at 6-10x.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Valuing on COVID-peak earnings. (2) Ignoring Wayfair-type cash burn.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| RH (Restoration Hardware) | 2016-2019 | Revenue decline, inventory issues, oversized stores underperforming | Membership model pivot, gallery format, content curation, real estate strategy | Successful | 3 years | Stock from $25 (2016) to $250+ (2019) |
| Wayfair | 2022-present | Post-COVID demand collapse, cumulative $4B+ losses, cash burn | Workforce cuts (20%), unit economics focus, breakeven targeting | In progress | Ongoing | Stock from $350 (2021) to $30-70 range |

**Pattern:** Furnishings turnarounds depend on housing cycle recovery and brand differentiation. RH is an exceptional outlier. 1 of 2 recovered. Base rate ~50%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical
- **Cyclicality:** High (tied to housing and consumer confidence)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare
