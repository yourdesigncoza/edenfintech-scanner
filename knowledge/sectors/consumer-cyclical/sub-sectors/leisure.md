# Leisure

## Overview

Leisure companies include fitness (Planet Fitness PLNT, Peloton PTON), toys (Hasbro HAS, Mattel MAT), outdoor/sporting goods (Vista Outdoor VSTO), and entertainment venues (Six Flags/Cedar Fair FUN, Topgolf Callaway MODG). Revenue is driven by consumer discretionary spending on entertainment and recreation. Business models range from subscription/membership (Planet Fitness) to product-based (Hasbro, Mattel) to venue-based (theme parks). The sector benefits from the "experiences over things" consumer trend but faces competition for consumer leisure time.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 15-25% (venue/subscription), 10-15% (product) | <8% |
| Same-Store/Membership Growth | Organic demand | 3-6% | Negative |
| Revenue Per Member/Visit | Customer monetization | Growing | Declining |
| Licensing Revenue % | Asset-light income | Growing for toy/entertainment | Declining |
| Attendance (venues) | Demand volume | Growing or stable | Declining 2+ years |
| Per Capita Spending (venues) | Pricing power | Growing above inflation | Flat or declining |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <3x | >5x |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 8-15% | <3% |
| Product Innovation Pipeline | Future revenue | Multiple launches per year | Stale product line |
| Customer Retention/Churn | Loyalty | <5% monthly churn (subscription) | >8% |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 10-16x for established operators. Subscription models (Planet Fitness) trade at premium (18-22x).
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Overvaluing COVID-era demand (Peloton). (2) Not adjusting for heavy capex in venue-based businesses.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Mattel | 2017-2022 | Toys R Us bankruptcy (lost key channel), margin collapse | New CEO Ynon Kreiz, IP licensing (Barbie movie), cost cuts, DTC | Successful | 4-5 years | Stock from $8 (2018) to $22+ (2023) |
| Peloton | 2022-present | Demand collapse post-COVID, $3B+ losses, CEO ousted | Cost restructuring, hardware price cuts, content focus, new leadership | In progress -- still burning cash | Ongoing | Stock from $160 (2021) to $4-8 range |

**Pattern:** Leisure turnarounds work when the brand/IP remains relevant and management right-sizes cost structure. Mattel/Barbie movie is exceptional. Peloton shows how COVID pull-forward creates false baselines. 1 of 2 recovered. Base rate ~50%.
