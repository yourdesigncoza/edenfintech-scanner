## Where Standard FCF Multiples DON'T Apply

1. **Auto Manufacturers** -- Captive finance arms (Ford Credit, GM Financial) distort FCF. Use P/E or sum-of-parts separating manufacturing and financial services.

2. **Residential Construction** -- Land acquisition and development creates extreme FCF lumpiness. P/B is the better cross-check. FCF in any single year is misleading.

3. **Cruise Lines** -- Extreme debt loads mean equity FCF is a tiny fraction of enterprise FCF. EV/EBITDA is the primary metric; equity value is highly leveraged to small changes in EBITDA.

4. **Distressed OEM Auto Parts Suppliers** -- When EBITDA is negative, EV/EBITDA is meaningless. Use EV/Revenue (0.3-0.6x) or asset-based liquidation value for the manufacturing plants.

5. **Casino OpCo/PropCo Structures** -- When casinos have separated operating companies from property companies (REIT structure), reported EBITDA excludes rent payments. Use EBITDAR (EBITDA before rent) for comparability, or adjust for the rent obligation.

## Cross-Reference with Valuation Guidelines

Per `knowledge/valuation-guidelines.md`, the Cyclical/Industrials baseline is 12-15x FCF. For Consumer Discretionary:

- **Aftermarket auto parts retailers** (AZO, ORLY) should be valued closer to Consumer Staples baseline (25-28x) due to non-discretionary demand characteristics. Discount schedule applies for individual company issues.
- **OEM auto parts suppliers** should use the lower end of Cyclical/Industrials (10-12x) or below (8-10x) for distressed names. Apply full discount schedule from valuation-guidelines.md.
- **Restaurants (franchise)** deserve a multiple between Cyclical and Consumer Staples depending on franchise mix.
- **All other sub-sectors** start from Cyclical/Industrials 12-15x baseline and adjust per the discount schedule.

## Discount Schedule Application (Consumer Cyclical Specifics)

| Condition | Standard Discount | Consumer Cyclical Notes |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| Revenue declining 2+ years | -3x to -5x | Common for auto OEM suppliers and department stores |
| Above-average leverage | -2x to -4x | Critical for casinos, cruises, auto suppliers |
| Regulatory/geopolitical risk | -2x to -3x | Tariffs (auto/apparel), gaming licenses (casinos) |
| Limited pricing power | -1x to -3x | Commodity apparel, mass-market electronics |
| Secular headwind | -2x to -4x | Department stores, some mall-based apparel retail |
