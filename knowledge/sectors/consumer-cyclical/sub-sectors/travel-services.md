# Travel Services

## Overview

Travel service companies include OTAs (Booking Holdings BKNG, Expedia EXPE), cruise lines (Carnival CCL, Royal Caribbean RCL, Norwegian NCLH), and other travel intermediaries. OTAs are asset-light platforms earning commissions (15-25% take rates) with high margins (EBITDA 30-40%). Cruise lines are extremely capital-intensive with high fixed costs, massive debt loads, and revenue that swings dramatically with demand. The COVID impact was devastating for cruises (zero revenue for 15+ months) but OTAs recovered faster.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Gross Bookings Growth | Demand | 5-12% annually | Declining |
| Take Rate (OTA) | Commission power | 13-18% | Declining (supplier disintermediation) |
| EBITDA Margin (OTA) | Profitability | 30-40% | <25% |
| Occupancy (Cruise) | Capacity utilization | 100-105% (double occupancy basis) | <90% |
| Net Yield (Cruise) | Revenue per capacity | Growing | Declining |
| Debt/EBITDA (Cruise) | Leverage | <4x | >6x (still elevated post-COVID) |
| EBITDA Margin (Cruise) | Profitability | 25-35% | <15% |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 20-30% (OTA), 5-12% (cruise) | Negative |
| Customer Acquisition Cost | Marketing efficiency | Stable or declining | Rising |
| Newbuild Order Book (Cruise) | Future capacity | 2-3 ships/year | >4/year (overcapacity risk) |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 15-25x for OTAs. 8-12x for cruise lines (reflecting leverage and capital intensity).
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Ignoring cruise debt loads (CCL carried $30B+ post-COVID). (2) Not adjusting OTA multiples for stock-based comp.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Royal Caribbean | 2020-2023 | Zero revenue 15 months, $20B+ debt, near-bankruptcy | Equity/debt raises, phased restart, pricing power maintained | Successful | 3 years | Stock from $20 (2020) to $200+ (2024) |
| Carnival | 2020-2024 | Zero revenue, $35B+ debt, massive dilution | Equity raises, asset sales, phased restart, pricing recovery | Partial -- revenue recovered but debt remains extreme | 4+ years | Stock from $8 (2020) to $20-25 range (dilution-adjusted) |

**Pattern:** Cruise turnarounds require demand recovery, pricing power retention, and patience. Massive dilution impairs per-share recovery. 1 of 2 fully recovered. Base rate ~50% (per-share basis).

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical (cruises), Operational (OTAs)
- **Cyclicality:** Extreme (cruises), Moderate (OTAs)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Moderate (maritime safety, DOT travel rules)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare but catastrophic (pandemic, maritime disaster)

### Kill Factors

1. **Pandemic-scale travel shutdown with no government support** -- cruises showed how zero revenue + fixed costs = existential

### Friction Factors

1. **Post-COVID debt overhang (cruises)** -- suggested friction: -1. Massive leverage limits recovery.
2. **Supplier disintermediation of OTAs** -- suggested friction: 0. Hotels/airlines building direct channels.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 (OTAs), 2/5 (cruises -- extreme leverage and binary pandemic risk)
**Suggested PCS friction:** 0 (OTAs), -1 (cruises)
