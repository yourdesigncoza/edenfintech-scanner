# Department Stores

## Overview

Department stores (Macy's M, Nordstrom JWN, Kohl's KSS, Dillard's DDS) operate large-format stores selling apparel, cosmetics, home goods. The sector faces severe secular headwinds from e-commerce, mall traffic decline, and consumer preference shifts to specialty/DTC brands. Revenue has declined structurally for most operators. Survivors are those with strong real estate (Dillard's owns many stores), off-price strategies, or successful digital transformation. Margins are thin (EBITDA 8-12%) and declining for most operators.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|

| Credit Card Revenue % | Ancillary income | 3-5% of revenue | Declining (tighter lending) |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 3-7% | Negative |
| Owned Real Estate Value | Asset floor | Material | Declining location quality |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 4-8x. Significant discount to other retail due to secular decline.
- **Alternative:** Sum-of-parts with real estate valued separately (relevant for Macy's, Dillard's).
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Ignoring secular decline trajectory. (2) Overvaluing real estate (not all locations are valuable). (3) Not adjusting for lease liabilities.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Dillard's | 2018-2024 | Declining revenue, underestimated by market | Massive buybacks (reduced share count 60%+), cost discipline, owned real estate | Successful (per-share value creation) | 5+ years | Stock from $55 (2018) to $400+ (2024) |
| JCPenney | 2011-2020 | Ron Johnson strategy failure, brand confusion | Multiple CEO changes, attempted repositioning | Failed -- Chapter 11 (2020) | 9 years of decline | Stock from $40 to $0 |
| Sears | 2010-2018 | Revenue collapse, asset stripping by Eddie Lampert | Store closures, real estate monetization, online pivot attempts | Failed -- Chapter 11 (2018) | 8+ years of decline | Stock from $100+ to $0 |

**Pattern:** Department store turnarounds are extremely rare. Dillard's succeeded through financial engineering (buybacks + owned real estate), not operating turnaround. Traditional operational turnarounds have failed consistently. 1 of 3 succeeded (and it was atypical). Base rate ~30%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational (secular decline)
- **Cyclicality:** Moderate
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Occasional (liquidity crises, bankruptcy)

### Kill Factors

1. **Secular decline + high leverage** -- the combination is fatal (Sears, JCPenney)
2. **No owned real estate + declining traffic** -- eliminates asset floor

### Friction Factors

1. **E-commerce secular headwind** -- suggested friction: -1. Structural and ongoing.
2. **Mall traffic decline** -- suggested friction: 0 to -1. Location-dependent.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 2/5 -- Secular decline makes outcomes hard to predict; "turnaround" usually means managed decline.
**Suggested PCS friction:** 0 -- Risk is operational, not regulatory/political.
