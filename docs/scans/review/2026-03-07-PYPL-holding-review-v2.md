## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** No. CEO Chriss was fired Feb 3, 2026 for insufficient execution pace. Q4 2025 revenue missed ($8.68B vs $8.80B expected). Branded checkout growth decelerated to 1% in Q4 from 6% a year prior. FY2025 full-year revenue $33.3B. FY2026 guidance dramatically below expectations: low-single-digit EPS change, 2027 targets withdrawn.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** Yes -- prior management. Under Chriss, the narrative was turnaround execution and product-led growth. The board disagreed with results and fired him. New CEO Lores started March 1, 2026 -- too early to assess execution. Lores has been on the PYPL board since ~2021 and chaired it since July 2024, so he was complicit in the Chriss hire and in setting the expectations Chriss failed to meet.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** Mixed signal. Operating margin improved to 19.7% (FY2025, highest since FY2020). But FCF margin dropped to 16.7% from 21.3% in FY2024. The gap is driven by working capital and timing; operating income growth is real. Transaction margin shrank 50 bps in Q4 to 46.5%. SBC continues declining (3.0% of revenue, down from 5.4% in FY2021) -- a genuine positive.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** Yes. Apple Pay, Stripe, Block, and Adyen continue expanding in payments and checkout. Branded checkout growth decelerated to 1% in Q4 -- a clear sign PYPL is losing competitive ground in its core moat. Stripe's acquisition interest (see below) may actually confirm that PYPL's asset base is valuable even if the standalone execution is failing.

5. **Which macro events actually matter?** (a) Online commerce growth rates -- moderating, hurting transaction volume growth. (b) Consumer spending health -- CFO Miller noted weakness in "lower and middle-income consumers." (c) Interest rate environment -- PYPL earns float income on customer balances. (d) Tariff/trade uncertainty dampening retail spending.

## New Developments Since v1

### 1. Lores CEO Start (March 1, 2026)

Enrique Lores officially started as President and CEO on March 1, 2026. Background: 6+ years as HP Inc. CEO, led HP/HPE separation, expanded into services and subscriptions. Board insider (member since ~2021, chair since July 2024). He was the board chair who oversaw Chriss's firing. Early statements emphasize "innovation culture" and "AI-enabled" strategy but no concrete plan yet.

**Assessment:** Lores is an operator and cost disciplinarian (positive for margins) but has no fintech track record. His HP background is services/subscriptions, which is somewhat relevant to PYPL's shift toward subscription-like payment platform revenue. The fact that he was board chair during the Chriss era means he owns partial responsibility for the current situation. The mid-2026 deadline from v1 remains the right checkpoint.

### 2. Stripe Acquisition Interest (February 24, 2026)

Bloomberg reported Stripe has expressed preliminary interest in acquiring all or part of PYPL. Stock jumped ~10% on the news. Multiple law firms and analysts cited potential 30-50% premium ($60+/share). Interest reported from both Stripe and "private equity consortiums."

**Assessment:** This is speculative but meaningful. It creates an informal price floor -- if the stock drops much below current levels, acquirer interest likely increases. A Stripe/PYPL combination would create the dominant global payments platform. However, antitrust review would be significant. This is NOT the primary thesis but it provides downside protection and represents asymmetric optionality.

### 3. Securities Fraud Class Action (Filed Feb 2026)

Goodman v. PayPal Holdings, Inc. (No. 26-cv-01381, N.D. Cal.) filed alleging material misstatements about projected revenue outlook and growth during the Chriss era (Feb 25, 2025 through Feb 2, 2026). Lead plaintiff deadline: April 20, 2026.

**Assessment:** Standard post-decline shareholder lawsuit. These rarely result in material settlements for companies of PYPL's size. The class period aligns with Chriss's tenure -- Lores is not implicated. This is noise unless it escalates into SEC enforcement or criminal referral, neither of which is evident.

## Holding Verdict

- **Decision: HOLD_AND_MONITOR**
- **Reason:** The thesis remains weakened but not broken. The business fundamentals are sound -- $33.3B revenue, $5.6B FCF, operating margin at a multi-year high (19.7%), declining SBC, aggressive share buybacks, and 16.2% ROIC. The base case forward CAGR of 36.6% clears the 30% hurdle. The stock trades near its mechanical floor (~$40.68 moderate floor), limiting downside. The primary risk is execution under a new CEO from outside fintech, with branded checkout severely decelerating. The Stripe acquisition interest provides additional asymmetric optionality. The v1 monitor deadline remains correct: if Lores has not articulated a credible fintech strategy with measurable targets by mid-2026, the thesis should be downgraded to BROKEN.

**Monitor items (prioritized):**
1. **Lores strategic plan** -- Expected Q1-Q2 2026. No credible strategy by mid-2026 triggers thesis downgrade to BROKEN.
2. **Q1 2026 branded checkout growth** -- If still 1% or declining, competitive erosion is accelerating.
3. **Stripe acquisition progress** -- Any formal bid changes the calculus entirely.
4. **Buyback pace** -- Continuation of $6B+/yr buyback critical for per-share value creation.
5. **Class action developments** -- Monitor for SEC involvement or criminal referral (unlikely but material).
