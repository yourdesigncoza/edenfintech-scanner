# Auto Dealerships

## Overview

Auto dealerships (AutoNation AN, Lithia Motors LAD, Penske Automotive PAG, Group 1 GPI) buy vehicles from OEMs and sell to consumers, earning margins on new/used vehicle sales, F&I (finance and insurance), and parts/service. The industry has consolidated significantly, with public dealership groups acquiring smaller operators. Parts and service is the highest-margin segment (~45-50% gross margin vs 5-8% for new vehicles), providing a recurring revenue floor. The franchise model gives OEMs significant power over dealer operations.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| New Vehicle Gross Margin | Pricing power on new cars | 4-8% | <3% (inventory glut) |
| Used Vehicle Gross Margin | Used car profitability | 6-10% | <5% |
| Parts & Service Revenue % | Recurring revenue mix | 12-18% of total revenue | <10% |
| Parts & Service Gross Margin | High-margin segment health | 45-55% | <40% |
| F&I Per Vehicle | Finance/insurance income | $2,000-3,000 per unit | <$1,500 |
| SGA/Gross Profit | Operating efficiency | 65-75% | >80% |
| Inventory Days Supply | New vehicle supply | 45-65 days | >80 days or <20 days |
| Same-Store Revenue Growth | Organic growth | 2-5% | Negative 2+ quarters |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <3x | >4x |
| Customer Pay Revenue Growth | Service demand | 3-6% | Negative |
| Floor Plan Interest/GPU | Financing cost per unit | <$500 | >$1,000 |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 8-12x or P/E 8-14x. Dealerships trade at moderate multiples due to cyclical new vehicle exposure offset by recurring service revenue.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Floor plan debt should be treated as operating (not financial) leverage. (2) Ignoring the parts/service annuity stream undervalues quality operators.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| AutoNation | 2008-2012 | Revenue collapsed during GFC, new vehicle sales plunged | Cost cuts, shift to parts/service focus, share buybacks | Successful | 3-4 years | Stock from $7 (2009) to $45+ (2013) |
| Sonic Automotive | 2008-2011 | Same-store sales declined sharply | Portfolio optimization, digital investments | Successful | 2-3 years | Recovered from $4 to $22 by 2012 |

**Pattern:** Dealership turnarounds follow auto cycle recovery. Parts/service provides floor. 2 of 2 recovered -- but driven by macro recovery, not management action. Base rate high (~80%+) given non-discretionary nature of service demand.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical
- **Cyclicality:** High (new vehicle sales), Moderate (parts/service)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal (franchise laws protect dealers from OEM disintermediation)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare

### Kill Factors

1. **OEM franchise termination** -- loss of franchise rights eliminates revenue source (rare but possible with brand exits)

### Friction Factors

1. **Interest rate shock** -- suggested friction: -1. High rates suppress new vehicle affordability and increase floor plan costs.
2. **EV direct-sales model threat** -- suggested friction: 0. Tesla/Rivian bypass dealers, but franchise laws protect traditional OEM relationships.
