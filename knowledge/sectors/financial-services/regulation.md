# Financial Services -- Regulatory Landscape

## Regulatory Bodies

| Regulator | Jurisdiction | Discretion Level | Key Enforcement Powers |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Federal Reserve (Fed) | Bank holding companies, state member banks, GSIBs, systemic risk | High | Cease & desist, fines, divestiture, membership revocation, stress tests (CCAR/DFAST) |
| OCC | National banks, federal savings associations | High | Capital directives, C&D orders, civil money penalties, prohibition orders |
| FDIC | Deposit insurance, state non-member banks, bank resolution | High | Insurance termination, C&D orders, receivership, civil money penalties |
| CFPB | Consumer financial protection (all financial products) | Moderate-High | UDAAP enforcement, civil penalties, supervisory exams for large nonbanks |
| SEC | Securities markets, broker-dealers, investment advisers, exchanges | Moderate | Civil enforcement, registration revocation, disgorgement, injunctions |
| FINRA | Broker-dealer conduct (self-regulatory) | Moderate | Fines, suspensions, bars, expulsions |
| State regulators | Insurance (primary), money transmission, banking (charter) | Varies | Rate approval, license revocation, surplus requirements |
| NAIC | Insurance standards coordination (no direct enforcement) | Low | Model laws adopted by states |

## Key Frameworks

- **Dodd-Frank Act (2010):** Created CFPB, established FSOC for systemic risk, Volcker Rule (proprietary trading restrictions), OLA (orderly liquidation authority)
- **Basel III / Basel III Endgame:** Capital adequacy standards. CET1 >4.5% minimum, plus capital conservation buffer (2.5%), plus GSIB surcharge (1-3.5%). Endgame re-proposal underway as of 2026.
- **CCAR/DFAST Stress Testing:** Annual Fed stress tests for banks >$100B assets. Results determine capital distribution capacity. Category I-IV framework determines testing intensity.
- **Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) / AML:** Anti-money laundering compliance. FinCEN enforcement. Violations carry severe penalties (TD Bank $3B+ settlement 2024).
- **Truth in Lending Act / Regulation Z:** Credit disclosure requirements enforced by CFPB.
- **State Insurance Regulation:** McCarran-Ferguson Act preserves state primacy. Each state has insurance commissioner. NAIC coordinates standards.

## Binary Regulatory Risks

1. **FDIC receivership / bank seizure** -- Occurs when capital is critically deficient or bank run is underway. Post-2023: SVB, First Republic, Signature seized within weeks.
2. **Fed asset cap** -- Revenue-constraining but not existential. Wells Fargo has operated under cap since 2018.
3. **CFPB late fee cap ($8 rule)** -- Could eliminate $10B+ in industry late fee revenue. Currently in legal limbo.
4. **DOJ antitrust action against card networks** -- Visa facing DOJ suit (Sept 2024). Historical settlements have been monetary, not structural.
5. **State insurance license revocation** -- Rare but existential for insurers in affected states.
