# Specialty Business Services

## Overview

Specialty business services companies classified under Consumer Cyclical by FMP include staffing (Robert Half RHI), education (Adtalem ATGE, Stride LRN), and consumer-facing services. Revenue is cyclical for staffing (tied to employment cycles) and more stable for education (counter-cyclical enrollment). Business models are generally asset-light with moderate margins (EBITDA 10-18%). This category is a catch-all for FMP and spans diverse business models.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Organic Revenue Growth | Demand | 3-8% | Negative 2+ quarters |
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 10-18% | <8% |
| Billing Rate/Bill-Pay Spread (staffing) | Pricing power | Expanding or stable | Compressing |
| Enrollment Trends (education) | Demand | Growing | Declining without external factors |
| Customer Retention Rate | Stickiness | >85% | <70% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2x | >3.5x |

| Contract Duration | Visibility | Lengthening | Shortening |
| Regulatory Compliance Status (education) | Risk | Current | Under investigation |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 10-16x for quality operators. Staffing at 8-12x (cyclical). Education at 10-15x.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Not adjusting staffing multiples for cycle position. (2) Education companies face regulatory overhang (gainful employment rules).

## Turnaround Precedents

Insufficient data -- the category is too diverse for meaningful precedent generalizations. Individual company precedents apply within specific sub-categories.

**Pattern:** No sector-level turnaround precedents -- analyst default base rate: 50%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational (staffing), Regulatory (education)
- **Cyclicality:** High (staffing), Low (education)
- **Regulatory discretion:** High for for-profit education (DOE gainful employment rules)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Occasional for education (Title IV funding loss)

### Kill Factors

1. **Title IV funding loss for education companies** -- eliminates ~90% of revenue for student-dependent operators

### Friction Factors

1. **Employment cycle downturn (staffing)** -- suggested friction: -1. Revenue declines 15-25% in recessions.
2. **Gainful employment rules (education)** -- suggested friction: -1 to -2. Regulatory discretion is high.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 (staffing -- cyclical but predictable) / 2/5 (education -- regulatory uncertainty)
**Suggested PCS friction:** 0 (staffing) / -1 (education)
