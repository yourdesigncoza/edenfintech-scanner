# Home Improvement

## Overview

Home improvement retailers (Home Depot HD, Lowe's LOW, Floor & Decor FND) sell building materials, tools, and home improvement products to DIY consumers and professional contractors (Pro segment). The sector benefits from aging housing stock and home price appreciation (homeowner wealth effect). HD and LOW are duopoly incumbents with massive scale advantages. Pro segment (40-50% of HD revenue) provides stability. Revenue is sensitive to housing turnover, home equity, and interest rates (fewer moves = fewer renovation projects).

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |

| ROIC | Capital efficiency | >35% | <20% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2.5x | >3.5x |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 8-12% | <6% |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** P/E 18-25x or EV/EBITDA 15-20x. Premium multiples justified by scale advantages, recurring demand, and Pro segment stability.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Assuming housing turnover sensitivity is the same as housing cycle sensitivity (it's not -- repair/remodel demand is more stable). (2) Not recognizing the duopoly premium HD/LOW deserve.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Lowe's | 2018-2022 | Underperforming vs HD, margin gap, operational inefficiency | New CEO Marvin Ellison (from HD/JCPenney), "Total Home" strategy, Pro focus, store resets | Successful | 3-4 years | Stock from $80 (2018) to $250+ (2022) |

**Pattern:** Home improvement turnarounds are primarily operational execution stories. The underlying demand is structurally sound. 1 of 1 recovered. Base rate ~80% (sector is resilient).

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational
- **Cyclicality:** Moderate (repair/remodel more stable than new construction)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Very rare

### Kill Factors

1. None identified -- duopoly structure provides enormous resilience

### Friction Factors

1. **Housing turnover decline** -- suggested friction: 0. Temporary impact, repair/remodel offsets.
2. **Tariff impact on building materials** -- suggested friction: 0 to -1. Can be passed through to consumers.
