## Cross-Sector Recovery Patterns

### Common Success Factors
1. **Management change with clear mandate** — nearly every successful turnaround involved a new CEO with authority to restructure (SFM/Jack Sinclair, BG/Greg Heckman, MDLZ/Dirk Van de Put, DLTR/Rick Dreiling)
2. **Portfolio simplification** — divesting underperforming businesses to focus and deleverage (STZ wine divestiture, SPB HHI sale, Hostess relaunch on core brands)
3. **Reinvestment in core brands** — companies that cut marketing spend to boost short-term earnings (KHC under 3G) faced longer, harder recoveries than those that maintained investment
4. **Pivot to growing category** — SAM to hard seltzer/tea, MGPI to premium branded spirits, STZ to Modelo/Corona. Category tailwinds provide recovery momentum.
5. **Balance sheet repair** — successful turnarounds prioritized deleveraging to restore financial flexibility

### Common Failure Factors
1. **Excessive leverage without operational support** — thin-margin businesses (grocery, dollar stores, distribution) cannot service 5x+ EBITDA debt
2. **Regulatory/legal existential risk realized** — when regulatory action materializes (ITT Tech, Corinthian), equity goes to zero
3. **Inability to differentiate** — undifferentiated competitors (A&P, 99 Cents Only) in commoditized markets have no recovery path
4. **Secular decline without adaptation** — companies that failed to transition to growing categories were gradually eroded (Revlon's brand irrelevance)

### Recovery Timelines
- **Fast (1-2 years):** Exogenous shock recovery (Sysco/COVID), operational fix (Clorox/cyberattack)
- **Medium (2-4 years):** Strategic pivot + operational improvement (SAM, SFM, BG, STZ)
- **Slow (4-6 years):** Deep restructuring + deleveraging (SPB, VGR, PM litigation era)
- **Failed:** Typically apparent within 2-3 years if leverage cannot be reduced or regulatory/competitive position deteriorates

### Overall Sector Base Rate
Across all 38 precedent cases compiled:
- **Successful:** 21 cases (~55%)
- **Partial:** 8 cases (~21%)
- **Total failure:** 9 cases (~24%)

**Adjusted turnaround base rate for Consumer Staples: ~55% success, ~20% partial, ~25% failure.** Analyst should use 60% as the default starting band for Consumer Staples turnaround candidates, adjusting up or down based on sub-sector-specific base rates and Likert modifiers.
