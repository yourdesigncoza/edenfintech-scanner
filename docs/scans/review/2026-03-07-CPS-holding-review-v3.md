## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** Yes. FY2025 adj. EBITDA margin hit 7.6% (up from ~4.3% in FY2024). Management guided 10%+ adj. EBITDA margin for 2026 ($260-300M on $2.7-2.9B revenue). Gross margin improved from 10.3% (FY2023) to 11.7% (FY2025). $298M in new business awards (74% EV/hybrid). Debt refinancing -- previously the key watch item -- has been completed.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** No. Management committed to margin expansion and delivered. FY2025 adj. EBITDA of $209.7M hit high end of guidance. Positive FCF for third straight year. The debt refinancing that was flagged as "near future" in v1/v2 has now been executed: $1.1B of 9.25% Senior Secured Notes due 2031 issued, fully redeeming 2026 and 2027 maturities. Management also set 2030 targets of mid-teen EBITDA margins.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** Margins expanding as expected on the full-year basis. Q4 2025 saw a dip (5.2% adj. EBITDA margin vs 8.2% prior year Q4) driven by Ford F-Series supply chain disruption -- customer-caused, not self-inflicted. Full-year gross margin trajectory: 3.7% (FY2021) -> 5.1% (FY2022) -> 10.3% (FY2023) -> 11.1% (FY2024) -> 11.7% (FY2025). Q3 2025 gross margin hit 12.5%. Management guiding 13%+ gross margin for 2026.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** No. CPS competes primarily in sealing, fuel/brake delivery, and fluid transfer systems. Tier 2 auto parts peers (Goodyear, AXL, DAN, VC) are not showing superior margin recovery trajectories. CPS's 74% EV/hybrid new business mix is differentiated. China OEM pivot (targeting 60% by 2030 from 36%) is a unique positioning angle.

5. **Which macro events actually matter?** (a) Auto tariffs -- 25% tariffs on imported autos/parts create supply chain uncertainty but CPS's global manufacturing footprint provides some flexibility. (b) Auto production volumes (SAAR) -- CPS revenue is volume-dependent. (c) Interest rates -- with the refi done at 9.25% for $1.1B, rate sensitivity is now fixed until 2031. This removes the single biggest near-term risk from v2.

| Year | Revenue | Gross Margin | Adj. EBITDA Margin | FCF | FCF Margin |

| FY2021 | $2.33B | 3.7% | -3.3% | -$212M | -9.1% |

| FY2025 | $2.74B | 11.7% | 7.6% | $16M | 0.6% |

- **Margin change:** Gross and EBITDA margins continue expanding. FCF margin is thin due to the $115M annual interest burden. With refinancing at 9.25% on $1.1B, annual interest will be approximately $102M -- a modest reduction. The real FCF unlock comes from EBITDA expansion: at 10% EBITDA margin on $2.8B revenue = $280M EBITDA, minus ~$102M interest, minus ~$50M capex = ~$128M potential operating cash flow, yielding meaningful FCF.
- **Competitor drift:** No competitive loss. CPS's margin recovery trajectory is above Tier 2 auto parts average. EV/hybrid award win rate is differentiated.
- **Open risks:**
  1. Leverage still elevated: $1.26B total debt, negative equity (-$83M), net debt/EBITDA ~5.1x -- but maturity wall eliminated through 2031
  2. Interest expense (~$102M/yr at new rate) consumes significant operating cash flow
  3. Revenue flat at ~$2.7-2.8B for 3 years -- turnaround is margin-driven, needs volume growth for full payoff
  4. Tariff/trade policy uncertainty for globally integrated auto parts supply chains
  5. Q4 2025 customer disruption (Ford F-Series) showed single-program concentration risk

## Worst-Case Floor Note

CPS has negative trough FCF margins (FY2021: -9.1%, FY2022: -4.2%) and negative equity (-$83M). The mechanical floor using strict trough inputs produces a negative floor price, which implies 100% downside risk. This is consistent with the scoring penalty applied in the 2026-03-07 scan report (downside 100%, position capped at 3%).

The business has moved substantially away from trough conditions (three consecutive years of positive FCF, EBITDA margin 7.6% and rising, debt refinanced to 2031). The 100% mechanical downside reflects the mathematical reality of negative trough FCF, not the current probability of total loss.
