## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **Interest rate sensitivity spans the entire sector.** Banks (NIM), life insurers (investment income), mortgage companies (origination volume), and even card networks (consumer spending correlation) are all affected by rate movements, though in different directions and magnitudes.

2. **Regulatory intensification post-2023.** The SVB/First Republic failures triggered proposed expansion of stress testing, deposit insurance reform, and enhanced liquidity requirements. Basel III Endgame re-proposal will increase capital requirements for large banks. CFPB is active on consumer finance (late fee cap, open banking).

3. **Technology disruption is reshaping competitive dynamics.** Fintech competition (Stripe, Square, neobanks) pressures incumbent payment processors and banks. Open banking rules may accelerate data portability. AI is being deployed in underwriting, fraud detection, and trading. But technology also creates new risk categories (cybersecurity, social media-accelerated bank runs).

4. **Fee compression is structural in asset management.** The secular shift from active to passive management continues. Alternative managers (private equity, credit, real estate) are the growth engine. Traditional active managers face existential pressure.

5. **Climate risk is emerging as a material factor for insurers and banks.** P&C insurers face rising catastrophe losses. Banks face CRE concentration risk in climate-vulnerable regions. Regulatory climate stress testing is under discussion.

## Macro Context (as of March 2026)

- **Interest rates:** Fed funds rate at elevated levels; market expecting gradual cuts. Higher rates benefit bank NIM but stress mortgage volumes and CRE valuations.
- **Credit cycle:** Consumer delinquencies rising gradually (credit card NCOs at ~4.5% industry-wide, up from ~2.5% in 2021). Not crisis-level but trending adversely.
- **CRE market:** Office vacancy rates elevated (~20% nationally). Regional banks with concentrated CRE exposure under pressure. Industrial and multifamily more resilient.
- **Regulatory posture:** Post-2023 crisis response still playing out. Basel III Endgame delayed but expected. CFPB under new leadership (2025 transition) introducing uncertainty.
- **Capital markets activity:** M&A and IPO volumes recovering from 2023 trough. Healthy for capital markets firms.

## Inter-Sub-Sector Dynamics

- **Banks vs. Fintechs:** Traditional banks compete with payment processors and neobanks for deposits and payments. Banks are building their own digital capabilities while fintechs seek bank partnerships (or charters).
- **Card Networks vs. Regulators:** DOJ antitrust action against Visa, ongoing merchant interchange fee litigation, and proposed CFPB late fee cap create regulatory overhang for the entire credit services ecosystem.
- **Insurance Brokers vs. Carriers:** Brokers benefit from both hard and soft markets (commissions scale with premiums in hard markets; in soft markets, brokers help clients shop for coverage). Brokers are structurally advantaged.
- **Asset Managers vs. Passive Revolution:** Traditional active managers (Franklin Templeton, Invesco) lose share to passive (BlackRock, Vanguard). Alternatives (Blackstone, Apollo, KKR) capture institutional flows.
- **Banks vs. Mortgage Companies:** Banks have pulled back from mortgage origination, creating opportunity for non-bank originators (Rocket, UWM). But non-banks lack deposit funding, making them more rate-sensitive.
