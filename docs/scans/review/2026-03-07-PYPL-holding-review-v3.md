## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** No. CEO Chriss fired Feb 3, 2026 for insufficient execution. Q4 2025 revenue missed ($8.68B vs $8.80B consensus). Branded checkout growth decelerated to 1% in Q4. 2027 financial targets withdrawn. FY2026 guided to low-single-digit revenue growth and mid-single-digit EPS decline.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** Yes, under prior management. Chriss promised product-led turnaround and AI-powered commerce but delivered decelerating growth and missed targets. New CEO Lores started March 1, 2026 -- too early to assess. Lores was board chair during Chriss's tenure, sharing accountability for the failed strategy.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** Mixed. Operating margin improved to 19.7% in FY2025 (best since FY2020) -- cost discipline is working. FCF margin dropped to 16.7% from 21.3% in FY2024, driven by working capital timing. Transaction margin shrank 50 bps in Q4 to 46.5%. SBC declining (3.0% of revenue, from 5.4% in FY2021) -- a structural positive. Net: operating profitability is improving, but transaction-level economics are under pressure.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** Yes. Branded checkout at 1% growth in Q4 is the clearest evidence. Stripe holds ~29% online payment processing share and growing rapidly. Apple Pay at 818M users dominates contactless. Adyen revenue growing 21%. PayPal retains 43-47% online processing share but branded checkout -- the higher-margin, moat-defining product -- is stalling.

5. **Which macro events actually matter?** (a) Interest rates: PYPL earns float income on ~$24B customer balances; rate cuts reduce this. (b) Consumer spending: CFO noted weakness in lower/middle-income consumers. (c) Online commerce growth rates moderating globally. (d) Tariff/trade uncertainty dampening retail spending.
