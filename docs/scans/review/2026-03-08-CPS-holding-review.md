## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** MIXED. FY2025 Adj EBITDA improved to $209.7M (7.6% margin) from $180.7M, but Q4 2025 margins regressed to 5.2% due to customer production disruptions and inflation. FCF dropped to $16.3M from $25.9M. 2026 guidance targets $280M Adj EBITDA (~10% margin) which would be a significant step-up. Debt refinancing completed March 2026, extending maturities to 2031 -- a major deleveraging catalyst achieved.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** LARGELY CONSISTENT. CEO Edwards (tenure since 2012) delivered on organizational restructuring (product-line focus from 2024), China growth pivot (74% of new business wins EV-related, targeting 60%+ China OEM share by 2030), and debt refinancing. However, Q4 2025 margin miss vs full-year trajectory raises execution questions for 2026 ramp.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** IMPROVING TREND WITH SETBACK. Full-year gross margin improved from 5.1% (FY2022) to 11.7% (FY2025), but the Q4 dip (5.2% Adj EBITDA margin vs 8.2% prior year Q4) signals vulnerability to volume/mix headwinds. Targeted 10%+ Adj EBITDA margin for FY2026 is ambitious given the Q4 trajectory.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** NO MATERIAL DRIFT. CPS retains #1 global position in sealing systems, #2 in fuel/brake delivery, #3 in fluid transfer. 8.3% market share in auto parts. $298M new business wins in 2025 with 74% EV/hybrid-related shows positioning for industry transition.

5. **Which macro events matter?** US light vehicle sales projected to decline to 15.6M in 2026 (from 16.1M in 2025). BEV adoption stagnating but hybrid growing. China OEM growth (15%+ CAGR 2025-2028) is a meaningful tailwind. Tariff/trade policy risk exists but not yet triggered for auto parts. Global light vehicle production growth flat to slightly negative in 2026.

## Management Consistency
- **What management said:** CEO Edwards committed to margin recovery through organizational restructuring, cost optimization, and pivot to EV/hybrid content. Targeted deleveraging to 2x net leverage by 2028. Guided for positive FCF and improving EBITDA margins.
- **What management did:** Delivered product-line reorganization in 2024. Completed $1.1B debt refinancing in March 2026 extending maturities to 2031. Adj EBITDA improved from $180.7M to $209.7M (FY2024 to FY2025). Generated positive FCF in FY2024 and FY2025. Won $298M new business with 74% EV-related. However, Q4 2025 showed margin regression and missed analyst EPS estimates.
- **Match/Mismatch:** MATCH on strategic execution and deleveraging. PARTIAL MISMATCH on near-term margin consistency -- Q4 2025 was a setback against the improving trend.
