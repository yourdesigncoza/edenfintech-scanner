## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** Partially. Margin expansion is real — gross margin improved from 5.1% (2022) to 11.7% (2025), and adjusted EBITDA rose to $209.7M. However, FCF margin remains thin (~0.6% in FY2025) due to heavy capex and interest burden. Management guides 10%+ EBITDA margin for 2026 ($260-300M adjusted EBITDA), which tracks with the margin recovery catalyst. FCF conversion remains the lagging piece.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** No mismatch detected. Management promised margin expansion and delivered it. Adjusted EBITDA improved $29M year-over-year. They identified 90%+ of their targeted $90M in manufacturing efficiencies. The debt refinancing goal is stated but not yet executed — this is the key watch item.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** Gross margin is expanding as expected. However, FCF margins are surprisingly thin relative to EBITDA margin improvement. The gap comes from $115M in annual interest expense and ~$48M capex. The interest burden is the primary margin drag that must be resolved through deleveraging or refinancing.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** Not conclusively. CPS operates in sealing, fuel/brake delivery, and fluid transfer — relatively niche areas. The 74% of new business tied to EV/hybrid platforms positions them for the transition. The pivot to Chinese OEMs (36% of China segment revenue, targeting 60% by 2030) is differentiated.

5. **Which macro events actually matter?** Tariffs (25% auto tariffs from April 2025), auto production volumes (SAAR), and interest rates (refinancing ability). The Ford F-Series production disruption in Q4 2025 was a direct hit. Tariff resolution by mid-2026 is a near-term catalyst or risk.

## Management Consistency

- **What management said**: Target margin expansion, manufacturing efficiencies of ~$90M, positive FCF, and double-digit EBITDA margin by 2026
- **What management did**: Delivered $209.7M adjusted EBITDA (beat original plan), positive FCF for third straight year, identified 90%+ of efficiency targets, secured $298M new business
- **Match/Mismatch**: Match — management has been credible on operational commitments. The debt refinancing statement ("near future") is the one item where action has not yet followed words.

## Margin / Competitor Drift

- **Margin change**: Gross margin expanding steadily (5.1% -> 11.7% over 3 years). EBITDA margin approaching historical norms. FCF margin compressed at 0.6% due to $115M interest expense on $1.26B total debt.
- **Competitor drift**: No significant competitive loss detected. CPS is winning EV/hybrid awards (74% of new business). China OEM pivot is differentiated strategy most Western Tier 1 suppliers are not executing.
- **Open risks**: (1) Debt refinancing execution risk — notes may become current by March/May milestones. (2) Tariff exposure, particularly Fluid segment. (3) Ford F-Series production volatility as key customer dependency. (4) Negative stockholders' equity (-$83M) persists.
