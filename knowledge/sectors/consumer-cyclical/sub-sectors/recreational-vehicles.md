# Recreational Vehicles

## Overview

RV manufacturers (Thor Industries THO, Winnebago WGO, REV Group REVG) and powersports companies (Polaris PII, Harley-Davidson HOG) produce discretionary big-ticket vehicles. Demand is highly cyclical and interest-rate sensitive. Margins are thin for RV manufacturers (EBITDA 6-10%) but better for powersports (10-15%). The industry saw a massive COVID demand surge (2020-2021) followed by a painful normalization with dealer destocking through 2023-2024.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 8-12% (RV), 12-18% (powersports) | <5% |
| Dealer Inventory Days | Channel health | 60-90 days | >120 (destocking needed) |
| Retail Registration Trends | End-market demand | Stable or growing | Declining 3+ months |
| ASP (Average Selling Price) | Mix/pricing | Rising with product mix | Declining on discounting |
| Backlog/Order Rate | Forward demand | Stable or building | Cancellations rising |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2.5x | >4x |
| FCF Conversion | Cash quality | >60% of net income | <30% |
| Market Share by Category | Competitive position | Stable | Losing share in core segments |
| Warranty Claims Rate | Product quality | Stable/declining | Rising trend |
| Floor Plan Interest | Dealer financing cost | Manageable | Rising sharply with rates |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 8-12x. P/E 10-15x. Cyclical multiples appropriate.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Valuing on peak-cycle earnings (COVID boom). (2) Ignoring dealer channel inventory buildup.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Winnebago | 2008-2012 | Revenue -40%, near-bankruptcy during GFC | Cost cuts, workforce reduction, product refresh | Successful | 3-4 years | Stock from $3 to $25+ |
| Harley-Davidson | 2009-2013 | Revenue -30%, aging demographic, dealer closures | "Surge" strategy, new models, international expansion | Partial -- recovered revenue but secular challenges remain | 3-4 years | Stock from $8 to $70 |

**Pattern:** RV/powersports turnarounds track economic cycles closely. 2 of 2 recovered, but Harley's shows secular demographic headwinds can limit recovery. Base rate ~70% tied to cycle recovery.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical
- **Cyclicality:** Extreme -- most discretionary of all auto-related sub-sectors
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare

### Kill Factors

1. **Dealer channel collapse** -- widespread dealer failures would eliminate distribution (rare, only in severe recessions)

### Friction Factors

1. **Interest rate sensitivity** -- suggested friction: -1. Big-ticket financed purchases, demand collapses with high rates.
2. **Post-COVID normalization hangover** -- suggested friction: 0 to -1. Elevated dealer inventories from 2020-2021 surge.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 -- Cyclical but well-understood demand drivers.
**Suggested PCS friction:** -1 -- Extreme cyclicality.
