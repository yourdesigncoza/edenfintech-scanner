## Where Standard FCF Multiples DON'T Apply

### Tobacco: Use FCF Yield / Dividend Yield Instead
Standard FCF multiples (25-28x Consumer Staples baseline) dramatically overvalue tobacco stocks. The business is in secular decline with significant litigation overhang and ESG-driven multiple compression. Appropriate range: 8-14x FCF, implying 7-12% FCF yield. Value tobacco as a declining annuity with binary regulatory risk, not a growth business.

### Grocery Stores: Use EV/EBITDAR (Lease-Adjusted) Instead
Grocery EBITDA understates the true cost structure because massive operating lease obligations are excluded. Always use EBITDAR (EBITDA + rent) and lease-adjusted leverage metrics. A grocer at 7x EV/EBITDA may be at 10x+ EV/EBITDAR — the latter is the true economic multiple.

### Agricultural Products: Use Mid-Cycle Earnings Instead of Trailing
Commodity processors' earnings can swing 50%+ peak-to-trough. Using trailing P/E or EV/EBITDA at cycle peaks makes stocks look cheap (7x) when earnings are about to decline 40%. Use 10-year average EBITDA as the denominator. If current EBITDA is > 130% of 10-year average, the stock is likely at cycle peak regardless of apparent valuation.

### Food Confectioners: Normalize for Cocoa Prices
Current (2024-2025) cocoa prices at $10,000+/ton are 200%+ above historical averages. Trailing earnings/FCF for chocolate companies are severely depressed. Valuation should model normalized cocoa costs ($3,000-$5,000/ton range) to assess through-cycle earning power. BUT: do not assume normalization is guaranteed — West African supply issues may be structural.

### Wineries & Distilleries: Adjust for Aging Inventory
Brown spirits companies (bourbon, whiskey) tie up working capital in aging inventory for 4-12+ years. This depresses reported FCF relative to economic earning power. Adjust FCF for involuntary inventory build (aging is an investment, not an expense) when valuing distillers. Conversely, if a distiller is liquidating aged inventory to generate FCF, that is value destruction disguised as cash generation.

## Discount Schedule Application (from valuation-guidelines.md)

Consumer Staples baseline: 25-28x FCF. Apply cumulative discounts:

| Condition | Discount | Commonly Applies To |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| Revenue/volume declining 2+ consecutive years | -3x to -5x | Tobacco, packaged foods with volume decline |
| Above-average leverage vs peers | -2x to -4x | UNFI, ENR, SPB, DLTR (Family Dollar debt) |
| Regulatory, geopolitical, or structural risk | -2x to -3x | Tobacco (FDA), Education (DOE), Agricultural (trade war) |
| Limited pricing power (commodity, private label pressure) | -1x to -3x | Private label foods (THS), commodity agriculture (ADM, BG) |
| Secular industry headwind | -2x to -4x | Tobacco (smoking decline), disposable batteries (ENR) |

**Example: Altria (MO)**
Baseline: 25-28x -> Volume declining (-4x) -> Regulatory risk (-3x) -> Secular decline (-3x) -> Litigation reserve (-2x) = 13-16x. Actual trading range: 8-12x. The additional discount reflects ESG exclusion and binary risk premium not fully captured by the schedule.

## Consistency Rule Guidance

Within a Consumer Defensive scan, expect the median FCF multiple to be 16-20x. The 5x deviation rule means:
- Any stock at > 25x needs explicit justification (growth premium — CELH, FIVE)
- Any stock at < 11x needs explicit justification (distress discount — MO, UNFI, BIG-like situations)
- Tobacco stocks will naturally cluster at the low end; do not artificially inflate their multiples to match the median
