# Travel Lodging

## Overview

Hotel/lodging companies include asset-light franchise/management models (Marriott MAR, Hilton HLT, Hyatt H, Wyndham WH, Choice Hotels CHH) and asset-heavy operators. Asset-light models earn fees from franchisees/owners, generating 50-65% EBITDA margins with minimal capex. Asset-heavy models (owning physical hotels) have lower margins but more revenue per property. RevPAR (revenue per available room) is the key demand metric. The sector recovered strongly from COVID and has been a beneficiary of the "experiences over things" consumer trend.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| RevPAR | Room revenue efficiency | Growing 2-5% annually | Declining 2+ quarters |
| ADR (Average Daily Rate) | Pricing power | Growing with or above inflation | Declining |
| Occupancy Rate | Demand volume | 65-75% (US average) | <55% |
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 50-65% (asset-light), 20-30% (asset-heavy) | Below peer group |
| Net Unit Growth | System expansion | 3-6% annual | <2% (slowing pipeline) |
| Pipeline as % of System | Future growth | 30-50% | <20% |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 30-50% (asset-light) | <20% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <3x | >5x |
| Loyalty Program Size | Customer stickiness | Growing | Stagnant |
| Fee Revenue % | Asset-light indicator | >90% for fee model | Declining toward asset-heavy |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 14-20x for asset-light (MAR, HLT command 18-22x). 8-12x for asset-heavy.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Not distinguishing asset-light from asset-heavy. (2) Ignoring Airbnb competitive impact on certain segments.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Marriott/Starwood | 2020-2022 | Revenue -75% during COVID, massive cash burn | Furloughs, cost cuts, maintained franchise relationships, recovered with travel demand | Successful | 2 years | Stock from $65 (Mar 2020) to $180+ (2022) |
| Hilton | 2020-2022 | Similar COVID impact | Asset-light model proved resilient, fast recovery | Successful | 2 years | Stock from $60 (Mar 2020) to $160+ (2022) |

**Pattern:** Lodging turnarounds are pandemic-driven (COVID is the only modern example). Asset-light models recovered faster. 2 of 2 recovered. Base rate ~90% -- but the precedent set is narrow (single-event driven).

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical
- **Cyclicality:** High (business/leisure travel sensitive to economy)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Moderate (FTC junk fee rules, local hotel taxes)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare (pandemic-level events)

### Kill Factors

1. **Prolonged travel shutdown (pandemic/war)** -- sustained revenue zero for 6+ months without government support

### Friction Factors

1. **Business travel secular decline** -- suggested friction: 0. Remote work reduces business travel but leisure offsets.
2. **Airbnb competition in leisure segment** -- suggested friction: 0. Differentiated customer base.

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 -- Demand is reasonably predictable absent black swan events.
