# Residential Construction

## Overview

Homebuilders (D.R. Horton DHI, Lennar LEN, PulteGroup PHM, NVR NVR, Toll Brothers TOL, Meritage Homes MTH) build and sell residential homes. Revenue is driven by housing demand (mortgage rates, household formation, housing supply deficit). The US has a structural housing shortage estimated at 3-5 million units, providing a long-term demand tailwind. Margins expanded significantly 2021-2024 due to supply constraints and pricing power. The industry is highly rate-sensitive: mortgage rates directly impact affordability and buyer demand. Builders use buydown programs (paying points to lower buyer rates) to maintain volume when rates are elevated.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|

| Land Supply (years) | Land bank adequacy | 3-5 years | <2 or >7 years |
| SGA/Revenue | Overhead efficiency | 8-11% | >13% |
| Monthly Absorption Rate | Community velocity | 3-5 homes/month | <2 |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** P/E 6-10x or Price/Book 1.2-2.5x. Builders trade at low P/E multiples due to cyclicality concerns.
- **Why NOT FCF multiples:** Land acquisition and development timing creates lumpy FCF. P/B is the cross-check.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Using peak-cycle earnings for valuation. (2) Not adjusting for land bank quality/location. (3) NVR's asset-light model (no land on balance sheet) deserves premium.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| D.R. Horton | 2007-2012 | Revenue -60%, GFC housing collapse | De-risked land positions, focused on entry-level, maintained volume | Successful | 4-5 years | Stock from $5 (2008) to $25+ (2013) |
| PulteGroup | 2007-2013 | Similar GFC impact, merger with Centex | Merger integration, asset-light pivot, cost discipline | Successful | 5-6 years | Stock from $4 to $20+ |
| Beazer Homes | 2007-2015 | Near-bankruptcy, accounting issues, GFC | Survived via cost cuts, deleveraging, smaller footprint | Partial | 7+ years | Stock never returned to pre-crisis levels |

**Pattern:** Homebuilder turnarounds follow housing cycle recovery. Companies with strong balance sheets (DHI, NVR) recover; overleveraged builders (Beazer) may survive but don't fully recover. 2 of 3 successful. Base rate ~65%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Cyclical
- **Cyclicality:** Extreme (directly tied to housing cycle and mortgage rates)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Moderate (local zoning, building codes, environmental review)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare (GFC-level events)

### Kill Factors

1. **GFC-scale housing collapse** -- only happened once in modern history but was devastating
2. **Speculative land bank with no demand** -- land impairments can destroy balance sheet

### Friction Factors

1. **Mortgage rate spike** -- suggested friction: -1. Directly compresses demand.
2. **Local regulatory delays** -- suggested friction: 0. Permitting and zoning can slow projects.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 -- Cyclicality is well-understood but timing of cycles is unpredictable.
**Suggested PCS friction:** -1 -- Extreme macro sensitivity (interest rates).
