## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** No. Product execution catalyst has stalled -- Q4 2025 revenue missed consensus ($8.68B vs $8.77-8.82B expected), 2027 financial targets were withdrawn, and FY2026 guidance is flat to slightly negative on transaction margins. The branded checkout growth narrative under Chriss did not materialize. Lores has been CEO for only 6 days as of this review.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** Mismatch. Chriss promised growth acceleration and set 2027 financial targets, but the board fired him for execution falling 'not in line with the board's expectations.' The salesforce was 'not adequately equipped' per lawsuit allegations. Lores has pledged 'accelerating innovation and execution' but has no track record in fintech. Actions are unproven.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** Mixed. GAAP operating margin expanded 19bp to 17.4% in Q4 2025. Full-year FCF was $5.56B (16.7% margin), down from $6.77B (21.3%) in FY2024 but above FY2023's 14.2%. SBC declined from $1.48B (FY2023) to $1.00B (FY2025), a positive. Margins are not deteriorating structurally but the FY2026 guidance for flat-to-negative transaction margin dollars is concerning.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** Partially. PayPal retains ~43-45% global online payment processing share but Stripe grew to 20-29% share and $19.4B revenue in 2025. Block/Square GPV grew 12% YoY. Stripe is gaining among developers and tech companies. PayPal's competitive moat (brand, network effects, 432M users) remains but share erosion is evident, particularly in the enterprise/developer segment.

5. **Which macro events actually matter to this business now?** Three macro factors are material: (1) Consumer spending/e-commerce growth drives transaction volumes -- low single-digit guidance reflects caution here. (2) Interest rates -- PayPal earns ~$517M in interest income on customer balances; rate cuts would reduce this tailwind. (3) Regulatory environment -- CFPB and payments regulation could constrain product innovation or create compliance costs.

Under Lores (March 2026): Pledged 'accelerating innovation and execution, focusing on clear priorities and disciplined delivery.'

## Margin / Competitor Drift
- **Margin change:** Operating margins stable: GAAP operating margin 19.7% in FY2025 vs 16.7% in FY2024 (improvement). FCF margin 16.7% in FY2025 vs 21.3% in FY2024 (decline, partly due to working capital timing). No structural margin deterioration. SBC declining as a percentage of revenue (3.0% vs 4.9% in FY2023).
- **Competitor drift:** Stripe growing faster and gaining enterprise/developer share (20-29% global online payment processing vs PayPal's 43-45%). Block/Square GPV +12% YoY. Adyen gaining in cross-border payments. PayPal remains the largest but is losing relative positioning in key growth segments.
- **Open risks:**
  - CEO Lores has no fintech/payments experience -- turnaround depends on his ability to recruit strong product/tech lieutenants
  - Securities fraud class-action lawsuits (deadline April 20, 2026 for lead plaintiff) -- financial impact likely limited but creates distraction and reputational risk
  - FY2026 guidance for flat-to-negative transaction margin dollars suggests pricing/competitive pressure
  - Stripe IPO potential could draw attention and capital away from PYPL as a fintech investment
  - Interest income ($517M in FY2025) at risk if Fed cuts rates
