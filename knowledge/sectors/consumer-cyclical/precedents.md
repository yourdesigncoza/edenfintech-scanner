## Cross-Sector Patterns

### Common Success Factors

1. **New management with relevant experience** -- Best turnarounds (Best Buy, McDonald's, Lowe's, AAP) begin with a CEO who understands the specific industry.
2. **Debt restructuring or elimination** -- Chapter 11 is a viable tool for OEM auto parts and casinos; leverage is the #1 killer.
3. **Simplification** -- Pruning brands (GM), simplifying menus (McDonald's), reducing store count (apparel), focusing product lines (Cooper-Standard).
4. **Channel adaptation** -- Shifting to DTC, digital, or services from wholesale dependence.

### Common Recovery Timelines

| Sub-sector Type | Typical Recovery | Minimum | Maximum |
|----------------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| Cyclical (auto, housing) | 3-5 years | 2 years (if cycle helps) | 7+ years |
| Operational (restaurants, retail) | 2-4 years | 2 years | 6 years |
| Structural (department stores) | Rarely full recovery | N/A | Managed decline |
| Leverage-driven (casinos, cruise) | 3-6 years | 3 years | 7+ years |
