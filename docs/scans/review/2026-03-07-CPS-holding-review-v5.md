## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** Yes. Margin recovery is the core catalyst and is materializing: gross margin climbed from 3.7% (FY2021) to 11.7% (FY2025). Adj. EBITDA margin rose from effectively 0% (FY2022) to 7.6% (FY2025), with management guiding 10%+ for FY2026 ($260-300M on ~$2.8B revenue). Debt maturity wall eliminated via $1.1B refinancing to 2031. $298M new business awards secured in FY2025.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** No. FY2025 adj. EBITDA of $209.7M hit the high end of guidance. Third consecutive year of positive FCF ($16.3M). Debt refinancing executed as committed. $64M in plant efficiency cost savings delivered. Management credibility remains high under CEO Edwards (13+ years tenure).

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** No on a full-year basis. Q4 2025 saw a temporary dip (5.2% adj. EBITDA margin vs 8.2% prior year Q4) driven by Ford F-Series customer production disruptions -- not self-inflicted. Full-year trajectory remains strongly positive. Management expects Q1 2026 to be weakest quarter before improving through the year.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** No. Tier 2 auto parts peers (Dana, American Axle, Modine, Visteon) are not showing superior margin recovery trajectories. CPS's 74% EV/hybrid share of new business awards and China OEM pivot (targeting 60% of China revenue from Chinese OEMs by 2030, up from 36%) are differentiated positioning moves.

5. **Which macro events actually matter to this business now?** Three items: (a) 25% auto parts tariffs -- effective since May 2025. S&P forecasts CPS revenue growing 1.8% in 2026 despite tariffs; management stated net tariff effect on profitability expected to be zero. Global manufacturing footprint provides flexibility. (b) Auto production volumes -- S&P projects global production flat to -1% in 2026; Asia Pacific (+4.5%) offsets weakness. US light vehicle sales projected down to 15.6M from 16.1M. (c) Interest rates -- no longer a direct risk; debt is fixed at 9.25% through 2031.

## Margin / Competitor Drift
- **Margin change:** Gross and EBITDA margins continue expanding year-over-year. FCF margin is thin (0.6% FY2025) because ~$115M annual interest burden absorbs most operating cash flow. Post-refinancing, annual interest ~$102M ($1.1B at 9.25%). The real FCF unlock depends on EBITDA scaling: at 10% adj. EBITDA margin on $2.8B = $280M EBITDA minus ~$102M interest minus ~$55M capex = meaningful FCF generation in 2026-2027.
- **Competitor drift:** No competitive loss observed. CPS margin recovery trajectory is above Tier 2 auto parts average. The EV/hybrid new business mix at 74% is a meaningful differentiator vs traditional ICE-focused peers.
- **Open risks:**
  - Leverage still elevated: $1.26B total debt, negative equity (-$83M), net debt/EBITDA ~5.1x -- but maturity wall eliminated through 2031
  - Interest expense ~$102M/yr at 9.25% limits FCF conversion until EBITDA scales further
  - Revenue has been flat at ~$2.7-2.8B for three years -- turnaround is margin-driven, needs volume/mix growth for full payoff
  - 25% auto tariffs create ongoing uncertainty; US light vehicle sales projected down to 15.6M from 16.1M in 2026
  - Q4 2025 customer disruption (Ford F-Series) exposed single-program concentration risk
  - Negative equity means traditional valuation anchors (P/B) are unavailable
