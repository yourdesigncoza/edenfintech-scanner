# Footwear & Accessories

## Overview

Footwear and accessories companies (Nike NKE, Tapestry TPR, Capri Holdings CPRI, Steven Madden SHOO, Crocs CROX, Wolverine World Wide WWW) design, market, and distribute shoes, handbags, and accessories. Strong brands command premium multiples due to pricing power and global distribution. DTC channel growth improves margins but requires marketing investment. The industry ranges from athletic (Nike, Under Armour) to fashion (Tapestry, Capri) to value (Crocs, Skechers).

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Gross Margin | Brand pricing power | 50-65% | <45% |
| EBITDA Margin | Operating profitability | 15-25% | <10% |
| DTC Revenue % | Channel quality | 35-50% | <20% with declining wholesale |
| Comparable Store Sales | Organic demand | 3-6% | Negative 2+ quarters |
| Inventory Turns | Working capital | 3-5x | <2x |
| Brand Heat Score | Consumer demand proxy | Positive search trends | Declining social/search engagement |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 10-15% | <5% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <2x | >3.5x |
| International Revenue % | Geographic diversification | 40-60% | Over-concentrated in single market |
| Marketing/Revenue | Brand investment | 8-12% | >15% (diminishing returns) |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** P/E 15-25x or EV/EBITDA 12-20x for branded players. Nike historically trades at 25-35x P/E.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Applying Nike-level multiples to brands without comparable global distribution. (2) Not discounting for fashion risk.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Under Armour | 2017-2024 | Revenue stagnation, margin collapse, accounting investigation | CEO return (Kevin Plank), brand repositioning, cost cuts | In progress | 7+ years | Stock from $50 (2015) to $6-8 range |
| Wolverine World Wide | 2022-present | Overleveraged post-acquisitions, PFAS litigation, inventory glut | Divested non-core brands, debt paydown, inventory cleanup | In progress | Ongoing | Stock from $30 (2022) to $10 range |
| Crocs | 2017-2020 | Declining relevance, store closures, CEO change | Product simplification, digital-first strategy, influencer marketing | Successful | 3 years | Stock from $4 (2017) to $100+ (2021) |

**Pattern:** Footwear turnarounds work when the brand retains authentic consumer connection. Crocs is the gold standard. Under Armour shows how brand dilution is hard to reverse. 1 of 3 fully successful. Base rate ~40%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational
- **Cyclicality:** Moderate
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare

### Kill Factors

1. **Brand death** -- irreversible consumer disengagement with no product innovation pipeline
2. **Litigation exposure exceeding annual revenue** -- PFAS-type environmental liability (Wolverine)

### Friction Factors

1. **Fashion/trend risk** -- suggested friction: 0. Normal for the industry.
2. **China/tariff exposure** -- suggested friction: -1. Significant sourcing from tariff-targeted countries.
