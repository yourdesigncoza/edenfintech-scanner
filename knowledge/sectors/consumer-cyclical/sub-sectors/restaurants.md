# Restaurants

## Overview

Restaurant companies span franchise-heavy models (McDonald's MCD, Yum Brands YUM, Restaurant Brands QSR) and company-operated chains (Darden DRI, Dine Brands DIN, Wendy's WEN). Franchise models are asset-light with 40-60% EBITDA margins from royalty streams. Company-operated models are capital-intensive with 12-18% EBITDA margins. Revenue driven by same-store sales growth, unit count, and digital/delivery adoption. Labor costs (25-35% of revenue) and food costs (28-35%) are the key variable expenses.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Same-Store Sales Growth | Organic demand | 2-4% | Negative 2+ quarters |
| Restaurant-Level Margin | Unit economics | 15-22% (company-operated) | <12% |
| Franchise Mix % | Asset-light indicator | 85-95% (franchise-heavy) | Declining |
| EBITDA Margin | Operating profitability | 35-50% (franchise), 12-18% (company) | Below peer median |
| Unit Growth Rate | Expansion pace | 2-4% net new annually | Negative (closures exceed openings) |
| Average Unit Volume (AUV) | Store productivity | Growing with inflation+ | Declining in real terms |
| Digital/Delivery Mix | Channel adaptation | 25-40% | <15% with no growth |
| Labor Cost % | Wage pressure exposure | 25-32% | >35% |
| Food Cost % | Input cost pressure | 28-33% | >36% |
| Debt/EBITDA | Leverage | <4x (franchise), <3x (company) | >6x / >4x |
| Franchisee Health | System sustainability | Same-store sales positive | Widespread franchisee distress |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** EV/EBITDA 12-18x for franchise-heavy (MCD, YUM trade at 18-25x). 8-12x for company-operated.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Not distinguishing franchise vs company-operated multiples. (2) Ignoring franchisee economics -- franchisor can look healthy while franchisees suffer.

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| McDonald's | 2014-2017 | Traffic declines, menu complexity, brand fatigue | "Turnaround Plan" under Steve Easterbrook: all-day breakfast, menu simplification, Experience of the Future remodels | Successful | 2-3 years | Stock from $90 (2014) to $170+ (2017) |
| Dine Brands (Applebee's/IHOP) | 2017-2019 | Applebee's: 6 consecutive quarters negative SSS, massive closures | Back to basics, value pricing, bar program, neighborhood marketing | Successful | 2 years | Stock recovered from $30 to $90+ |
| Papa John's | 2018-2021 | Founder controversy, declining sales, brand damage | New CEO, Shaq as board member/ambassador, menu/operations improvements | Successful | 3 years | Stock from $30 (2018) to $130+ (2021) |

**Pattern:** Restaurant turnarounds succeed with new leadership, menu simplification, and operational back-to-basics. Franchise models are more resilient. 3 of 3 recovered. Base rate ~75% for branded chains.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational
- **Cyclicality:** Low (QSR) to Moderate (casual dining)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Moderate (health codes, minimum wage laws)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare (food safety crisis is the main binary risk)

### Kill Factors

1. **Widespread foodborne illness outbreak tied to systemic process failure** -- destroys consumer trust (Chi-Chi's/hepatitis A is the extreme case)
2. **Franchisee rebellion / system-wide financial distress** -- inability to support franchise obligations

### Friction Factors

1. **Minimum wage increases** -- suggested friction: -1. Particularly for company-operated chains.
2. **Consumer trade-down from casual to QSR** -- suggested friction: 0 to -1. Affects casual dining more.

## Epistemic Profile

**Epistemic stability:** 3/5 -- Restaurant demand is relatively predictable; execution risk is the main variable.
**Suggested PCS friction:** 0
