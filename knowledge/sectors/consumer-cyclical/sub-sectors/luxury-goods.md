# Luxury Goods

## Overview

Luxury goods companies listed on NYSE are primarily European-headquartered with US listings or US-based premium brands. Includes jewelry (Tiffany/LVMH), watches, premium fashion. Revenue depends on aspirational consumer spending, tourism, and Chinese consumer demand. Margins are exceptionally high (EBITDA 25-40%) due to brand-driven pricing power. The sector is resilient to mild recessions but vulnerable to severe downturns and geopolitical disruption of Chinese demand.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| Gross Margin | Pricing power | 60-75% | <55% (brand dilution) |
| EBITDA Margin | Profitability | 25-40% | <20% |
| Organic Revenue Growth | Demand health | 5-10% | Negative (rare for true luxury) |
| Price/Mix Contribution | Pricing power vs volume | >50% of growth from price/mix | Volume-driven = commoditization |
| DTC % | Channel control | 50-70% | <40% |
| China Revenue % | Geographic risk | 20-35% | >40% (concentration risk) |
| Comparable Store Sales | Store productivity | 5-10% | Negative |
| Marketing/Revenue | Brand investment | 8-12% | <5% (underinvesting) or >18% |
| FCF Margin | Cash generation | 15-25% | <10% |
| Inventory Turns | Product freshness | 2-4x | <1.5x |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** P/E 20-35x or EV/EBITDA 18-30x. Premium justified by extraordinary margins and brand durability.
- **Common pitfalls:** (1) Not distinguishing "true luxury" from "accessible luxury" (which deserves lower multiples). (2) Ignoring China exposure risk.

## Turnaround Precedents

Insufficient data -- true luxury brands rarely experience fundamental distress; they tend to be acquired (Tiffany by LVMH) rather than turnaround independently. "Accessible luxury" brands (Coach/Tapestry, Michael Kors/Capri) have turnaround precedents but are better classified under Footwear & Accessories.

**Pattern:** No turnaround precedents -- analyst default base rate: 50%.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Operational (brand management)
- **Cyclicality:** Moderate (true luxury is recession-resistant, accessible luxury is cyclical)
- **Regulatory discretion:** Minimal
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Rare

### Kill Factors

1. **Brand damage from scandal/counterfeiting at scale** -- irreversible if consumer trust collapses

### Friction Factors

1. **China demand shock** -- suggested friction: -1. Geopolitical tensions or Chinese consumer downturn.
2. **Currency headwinds** -- suggested friction: 0. Normal for global luxury but compresses reported growth.
