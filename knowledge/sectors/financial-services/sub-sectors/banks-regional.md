# Regional Banks

## Overview

Regional banks operate primarily within specific US geographic markets, with total assets typically ranging from $10B to $250B. Unlike diversified banks, they are more concentrated in traditional lending (commercial real estate, C&I, consumer) and deposit-taking, with limited or no investment banking, trading, or global operations. Revenue is dominated by net interest income (65-80% of total), making them highly sensitive to interest rate movements.

Key characteristics: higher CRE concentration than diversified banks (often 200-400% of risk-based capital vs <100% for large banks), stronger community relationships and local market knowledge, lower fee income diversification, and greater vulnerability to geographic/economic shocks in their footprint. The 2023 banking crisis (SVB, First Republic, Signature) revealed acute vulnerabilities: uninsured deposit concentration, held-to-maturity (HTM) bond portfolios with unrealized losses, and social media-accelerated bank runs.

Competitive dynamics are shifting: post-2023 deposit flight toward TBTF institutions, rising regulatory requirements (proposed Category III/IV stress testing expansion), and fintech competition for deposits and lending. Barriers to entry remain high (capital requirements, charter approval) but franchise value is eroding for banks without clear specialization.

## Key Metrics & KPIs

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|----------|
| CET1 Ratio | Core capital adequacy | >10% | <8% triggers supervisory action |
| NIM (Net Interest Margin) | Lending spread | 3.0-4.0% | <2.5% signals margin compression |
| CRE Concentration | CRE loans / risk-based capital | <300% | >300% draws regulatory scrutiny (interagency guidance) |
| Uninsured Deposit % | Funding vulnerability | <40% | >50% signals bank run risk (SVB had ~94%) |
| NCO Rate | Annual credit losses | 0.2-0.6% | >1.0% is elevated for regionals |
| NPL Ratio | Impaired loans / total loans | <1% | >3% is crisis territory |
| Loan-to-Deposit Ratio | Funding stability | 75-90% | >100% signals reliance on wholesale funding |
| HTM Unrealized Losses / Equity | Hidden balance sheet risk | <20% | >50% signals SVB-type vulnerability |
| ROTCE | Return on tangible equity | 10-15% | <8% suggests P/TBV should be <1.0x |
| Efficiency Ratio | Cost discipline | 55-65% | >70% signals operational bloat |
| Deposit Cost | Rate paid on deposits | Market-dependent | Rising faster than loan yields |
| Tangible Book Value per Share | Equity anchor | Growing 5-8% CAGR | Declining TBV is value destruction |
| Non-Interest Income % | Revenue diversification | 20-35% | <15% means pure rate dependency |
| Office CRE % of Loans | Sector concentration risk | <10% | >15% in current remote-work environment |

## Valuation Approach

- **Primary method:** P/TBV -- Regional banks typically trade 1.0-1.5x P/TBV. Premium regionals (M&T, Huntington) may reach 1.5-2.0x. Distressed regionals trade 0.5-0.9x.
- **Secondary method:** P/E -- Typical range 8-12x for regionals, vs 10-16x for diversified banks. Acquisition multiples can be 1.5-2.0x TBV.
- **Why NOT FCF multiples:** Same as diversified banks -- bank operations/financing are intertwined; FCF is meaningless for banks.
- **Typical multiple range:**
  - Quality regionals (M&T, CFG, HBAN): 1.2-1.8x P/TBV, 10-13x P/E
  - Average regionals (KEY, RF, ZION): 1.0-1.4x P/TBV, 8-11x P/E
  - Troubled regionals: 0.5-0.9x P/TBV, 5-8x P/E
- **Common pitfalls:**
  - Ignoring CRE concentration risk (particularly office exposure)
  - Assuming P/TBV <1.0x means "cheap" without checking ROTCE
  - Underestimating deposit flight risk in rising-rate environments

## Turnaround Precedents

| Company | Period | Situation | Actions Taken | Outcome | Timeline | Stock Recovery |
|---------|--------|-----------|--------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| Synovus | 2009-2013 | NPL >5%, capital ratio 6.66%, MOU requiring 8% leverage, $968M TARP | Raised $1.7B equity (2009-2010), consolidated 30 charters into 1, cut 850 jobs, closed branches | Full recovery. Repaid TARP July 2013. NPA ratio fell from >5% to 3.57% by 2012 | ~4 years | Stock from ~$1.50 (2009) to $25+ by 2014 |
| Zions Bancorp | 2008-2012 | NPAs rose 6x in 12 months, $1.4B TARP, tangible equity 5.97%, FinCEN $8M penalty (BSA violations) | Repaid TARP in 2 tranches (March + Sept 2012), consolidated 7 charters into 1 (2015), improved risk management | Full recovery | ~4 years | Stock from ~$8 (2009 trough) to $30 by 2014 |
| KeyCorp | 2008-2012 | Significant credit losses from GFC, $2.5B TARP, elevated NPLs, restructuring charges | Repaid TARP, refocused on relationship banking, reduced CRE concentration, improved efficiency | Full recovery, became acquirer (First Niagara 2016) | ~4 years | Stock from ~$4 (2009) to $14 by 2013 |
| First Republic | 2023 | Uninsured deposits >67%, $100B+ deposit outflow in days, HTM losses | Attempted rescue by consortium of 11 banks ($30B deposit), failed | FDIC seized, sold to JPM for $10.6B. Shareholders wiped out | Days (binary failure) | Stock went to $0 |
| SVB Financial | 2023 | Uninsured deposits ~94%, $42B deposit run in 1 day, HTM losses ($15B unrealized) | Attempted $2.25B equity raise, triggered panic | FDIC seized March 10, 2023. Shareholders wiped out | 48 hours | Stock went to $0 |

**Pattern:** Regional bank turnarounds from credit distress (GFC-era) have a good track record: 3 of 3 credit-stressed banks recovered within ~4 years. However, the 2023 failures (SVB, First Republic) show that liquidity-driven failures are binary and unsurvivable -- there is no turnaround from a bank run. The critical distinction: credit stress = gradual, potentially recoverable; liquidity crisis = binary, fatal. Analyst base rate: 60% for credit-stressed regionals, 0% for liquidity-crisis regionals.

## Risk Profile

- **Default risk type:** Financial (credit cycle) + Regulatory
- **Cyclicality:** High -- more concentrated loan books amplify cycle impact
- **Regulatory discretion:** High -- Fed, OCC, FDIC, state regulators; post-2023 proposals to expand stress testing to smaller banks
- **Macro sensitivity:**
  - Interest rates: HIGH (more rate-sensitive than diversified due to NII dominance)
  - CRE cycle: HIGH (concentrated exposure, especially office)
  - Unemployment: HIGH (drives consumer and small business defaults)
  - Deposit competition: HIGH (compete against TBTF for deposits post-2023)
- **Binary trigger frequency:** Occasional-to-Frequent -- 2023 showed regional banks face existential deposit flight risk; 3 large regionals failed in weeks

### Kill Factors (hard reject -- maps to enrichment override triggers)

1. **Uninsured deposits >60% with concentrated depositor base** -- SVB had 94% uninsured deposits concentrated in VC/tech. This is the primary bank run trigger. No turnaround is possible once deposit flight begins.
2. **HTM unrealized losses >50% of equity** -- Hidden balance sheet impairment that becomes real if forced to sell. SVB's $15B in unrealized losses on a $16B equity base was the proximate cause of failure.
3. **CRE concentration >400% of risk-based capital with rising vacancy rates** -- Regulatory guidance warns at 300%. Above 400% with deteriorating fundamentals signals concentrated credit risk.
4. **Active bank run or >10% deposit outflow in a quarter** -- Once started, self-reinforcing. No recovery case exists post-run.

### Friction Factors (PCS modifiers -- reduce confidence, don't kill thesis)

1. **CRE concentration 300-400%** -- suggested friction: -1, rationale: within regulatory warning zone but manageable if diversified by property type and geography.
2. **CAMELS downgrade to 3** -- suggested friction: -1, rationale: increased supervisory attention but not yet enforcement action territory.
3. **Office CRE >10% of loans** -- suggested friction: -1, rationale: remote work trend creates structural vacancy risk; watch maturity schedule.
4. **Recent acquisition integration** -- suggested friction: -1, rationale: integration risk and potential for hidden asset quality issues in acquired portfolio.

## Thesis-Breaking Patterns

- **Failure mode 1: Bank run** -- Deposit flight accelerated by social media. Binary (hours-to-days). Examples: SVB March 2023, First Republic March-May 2023, Signature Bank March 2023.
- **Failure mode 2: CRE credit cycle** -- Concentrated CRE exposure (especially office) leads to provision spike. Gradual over 1-3 years. Examples: S&L crisis 1980s, numerous community bank failures 2008-2012.
- **Failure mode 3: NIM compression death spiral** -- Inverted yield curve + deposit competition squeezes margins to unprofitability. Gradual. Examples: many regionals 2022-2023 as deposit costs surged.
- **Failure mode 4: Regulatory escalation** -- MOU -> consent order -> C&D -> receivership. Gradual escalation over 1-3 years. Examples: numerous community bank failures during S&L crisis.

## Evidence Requirements

### Must-Have Data Points

| Data Point | Where to Find It | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| CET1 Ratio | 10-K, FMP Key Metrics, Federal Reserve Y-9C | Regulatory capital floor |
| Uninsured Deposit % | 10-K Item 6, earnings call disclosures, FDIC Call Reports | Primary bank run vulnerability metric |
| CRE / Risk-Based Capital | 10-K Item 7, earnings supplements | Concentration risk; >300% draws interagency scrutiny |
| HTM Securities Unrealized Losses | 10-K Item 8 (Notes to Financial Statements), 10-Q | Hidden balance sheet risk post-2023 |
| NPL Ratio + Trend | 10-K, FDIC Call Reports, FMP metrics | Credit quality trajectory |
| Office CRE Exposure | Earnings supplements, investor presentations | Secular risk from remote work |
| NIM Trend (quarterly) | Earnings releases, FMP ratios | Direction matters more than level |
| Deposit Mix (DDA/savings/time) | 10-K, earnings supplements | Low-cost deposit franchise value |
| Tangible Book Value per Share | FMP balance sheet | Core valuation anchor |
| CAMELS or MOU/consent order status | FDIC/OCC enforcement databases (public for formal actions) | Regulatory health signal |

### Red Flags If Missing

- If a bank does not disclose uninsured deposit percentage, assume it is elevated
- If CRE exposure by property type (office vs industrial vs multifamily) is not broken out, analyst cannot assess structural risk
- If HTM unrealized losses are not disclosed, cross-reference FDIC Call Report data

## Epistemic Profile

| PCS Question | Typical Answer | Rationale |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Q1: Operational risk? | No | Risk is credit cycle + liquidity + regulatory, not operational execution |
| Q2: Regulatory minimal? | No | Multiple overlapping regulators; post-2023 proposals expand requirements |
| Q3: Historical precedent? | Yes | Strong credit-stress turnaround precedents (Synovus, Zions, KeyCorp); binary failure precedents (SVB, FRC) |
| Q4: Non-binary outcome? | Mixed | Credit stress is gradual; liquidity crisis is binary. Must assess which mode applies |
| Q5: Macro/geo limited? | No | Highly exposed to rates, credit cycle, and local economic conditions |

**Epistemic stability:** 2/5 -- Regional banks have lower predictability than diversified banks due to concentrated loan books, geographic dependence, and the post-2023 revelation that bank runs can happen at shocking speed via social media. The analyst must distinguish between credit-stressed (recoverable) and liquidity-stressed (binary) scenarios.

**Suggested PCS friction:** -1 -- Base case. Increase to -2 if bank has elevated uninsured deposits (>50%) or CRE concentration (>300%), as these shift the risk profile toward binary outcomes.
