## Thesis-Integrity Checklist

1. **Are catalysts showing up on schedule?** Yes. Margin recovery is the core catalyst, and it is materializing: gross margin has climbed from 3.7% (FY2021) to 11.7% (FY2025). Adj. EBITDA margin rose from effectively 0% (FY2022) to 7.6% (FY2025). Management guided 10%+ adj. EBITDA margin for FY2026 ($260-300M on $2.7-2.9B revenue). The debt maturity wall -- the single biggest risk from earlier reviews -- was eliminated via $1.1B refinancing on March 4, 2026, pushing maturities to 2031.

2. **Is management saying one thing, doing another?** No. FY2025 adj. EBITDA of $209.7M hit the high end of guidance. Positive FCF for the third consecutive year ($16.3M in FY2025). Debt refinancing executed as promised. $298M in new business awards secured. $64M in plant efficiency cost savings delivered. Management credibility remains high.

3. **Are margins shifting unexpectedly?** No. The margin expansion trend is firmly in place on a full-year basis. Q4 2025 saw a temporary dip (5.2% adj. EBITDA margin vs 8.2% prior year Q4) driven by customer production disruptions (Ford F-Series, not self-inflicted). Full-year trajectory remains strongly positive.

4. **Are competitors pulling ahead?** No. CPS competes in sealing, fuel/brake delivery, and fluid transfer systems. Tier 2 auto parts peers (Dana, American Axle, Modine, Visteon) are not showing superior margin recovery trajectories. CPS's 74% EV/hybrid share of new business awards and its China OEM pivot (targeting 60% by 2030) are differentiated positioning moves.

5. **Which macro events actually matter?** Three items: (a) **25% auto parts tariffs** -- effective since May 2025. S&P forecasts CPS revenue growing 1.8% in 2026 despite tariffs; management stated "muted effect" and "well-positioned to manage through." Global manufacturing footprint provides flexibility. (b) **Auto production volumes** -- S&P projects global production flat to -1% in 2026; Asia Pacific (+4.5% growth) offsets. (c) **Interest rates** -- no longer a risk factor; debt is fixed at 9.25% through 2031.

## Margin / Competitor Drift

### 5-Year Margin and FCF Trajectory

| Year | Revenue | Gross Margin | Adj. EBITDA Margin | Operating Income | FCF | FCF Margin |
|------|---------|-------------|-------------------|--------------------|-----|------------|
| FY2021 | $2.33B | 3.7% | -3.3% | -$209M | -$212M | -9.1% |
| FY2022 | $2.53B | 5.1% | 0.0% | -$105M | -$107M | -4.2% |
| FY2023 | $2.82B | 10.3% | 1.6% | $46M | $37M | 1.3% |
| FY2024 | $2.73B | 11.1% | 4.3% | $70M | $26M | 0.9% |
| FY2025 | $2.74B | 11.7% | 7.6% | $107M | $16M | 0.6% |

- **Margin change:** Gross and EBITDA margins continue expanding year-over-year. FCF margin is thin because the ~$115M annual interest burden absorbs most operating cash flow. Post-refinancing, annual interest will be approximately $102M ($1.1B at 9.25%), a modest improvement. The real FCF unlock depends on EBITDA scaling: at 10% adj. EBITDA margin on $2.8B revenue = $280M EBITDA, minus ~$102M interest, minus ~$55M capex = meaningful FCF generation.
- **Competitor drift:** No competitive loss observed. CPS's margin recovery trajectory is above Tier 2 auto parts average. The EV/hybrid new business mix at 74% is a meaningful differentiator.
- **Open risks:**
  1. Leverage still elevated: $1.26B total debt, negative equity (-$83M), net debt/EBITDA ~5.1x -- but maturity wall eliminated through 2031
  2. Interest expense ~$102M/yr at 9.25% limits FCF conversion until EBITDA scales further
  3. Revenue has been flat at ~$2.7-2.8B for three years -- turnaround is margin-driven, needs volume/mix growth for full payoff
  4. 25% auto tariffs create ongoing uncertainty; S&P projects flat-to-down global production for 2026
  5. Q4 2025 customer disruption (Ford F-Series) exposed single-program concentration risk
